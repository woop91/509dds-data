The Missouri Association of Disability Examiners

Constitution and Bylaws

(last revised 8/2005)

Article I Name of Association

The name of this organization shall be The Missouri Association of Disability Examiners (hereinafter referred to in this document as the “Association”).

Article II Status of the Association

The Association will be subject to the constitution and Bylaws of the National Association of Disability Examiners.

Article III Purpose of the Association

The Association shall foster and promote professional growth and implementation of the goals of the National Association of Disability Examiners.

Article IV Membership in the Association

The membership of this Association shall consist of the National Association of Disability Examiners members who reside or work in the state.

Article V Officers of the Association

A. The officers of the Association shall be: President, President-Elect, Secretary- Historian, Treasurer and Conference Manager.

B. The President-Elect shall be elected at the Annual Meeting and shall assume

the office of President at the beginning of the National Association of Disability Examiners annual meeting in the fall after the Annual Meeting. In the event of a vacancy in the office of President, the President-Elect shall act as President until such time as the vacancy is filled by the Board of Directors.

C. The Past Presidents shall serve as a member of the Board of Directions until the beginning of the National Association of Disability Examiners annual meeting in the fall next following the expiration of his term as President.

D. The Secretary-Historian shall be elected at the Annual Meeting for a term of one year during the time period specified under B and will be responsible for maintaining records of correspondence, minutes, and other documentation of historical significance to the Association.

E. The Treasurer shall be elected at the Annual Meeting for a term of one year during the time period specified under B and will be responsible for proposing an annual budget for Board approval.

F. The duties of the officers shall be those usually prescribed to such offices, and any specific duties as assigned by the Constitution and Bylaws.

G. The Conference Manager shall be elected at the Annual Meeting for a term of one year during the time period specified under B and will be responsible for organizing the annual Meeting and other conferences the Association may sponsor.

Article VI Board of Directors

1. The Board of Directors shall consist of the officers and Board Members at Large.
2. The Board of Directors shall conduct the business of the Association between

C. The Board of Directors shall, though election by the Board, fill vacancies in offices between Annual Meetings.

D. Meetings of the Board shall be called, as necessary, by the President, or by a majority of the Board.

E. Six members of the Board shall constitute a quorum.

F. The President, President-Elect, and Treasurer shall constitute the Executive Committee which shall conduct the business of the Association between meetings of the Board and report transactions at the next Board Meeting.

Article VII Committee of the Association

1. The President of the Association shall appoint an audit committee of two or more people to audit the financial records of the Association. This committee shall prepare and present a report for approval by the Association at the Annual Meeting.
2. The President of the Association shall appoint any necessary committees.

Article VIII Meetings of the Association

1. There shall be an Annual Meeting of the Association.
2. Other meetings of the Association may be called, as necessary, by the President.

Article IX Elections

A. Elections of Officers and Board Members at Large shall be by secret ballot. A plurality of votes for each position shall constitute election.

B. Officers will be elected at the Annual Meeting of the Association. Each member shall have one vote for each elected position.

C. In any member is unable to be present at the Annual Meeting, the member may provide a written proxy to another member who will be present. The written proxy must contain the following concepts, “I (printed name of the member) appoint (printed name of attending member/proxy) as my proxy to cast my ballot for the Missouri Association of Disability Examiners officers for (year).” Upon presentation of the proxy to a member of the Elections Committee at the Annual Meeting, the member carrying the proxy may cast the ballot of the absent member.

D. The Nominating Committee will provide a list of nominees to the Elections Committee at least two weeks prior to the Annual Meeting.

E. The membership in each District Office will select one Board Member at Large prior to the Annual Meeting and may select a replacement if the position is vacated between Annual Meeting.

F. Nominations will also be accepted from the floor at the time of the Annual Meeting. The official ballots will be prepared by the Election Committee and space will be made available on the ballots to write in names of anyone nominated from the floor.

G. Association membership will be determined by the most current membership list obtained from the chairperson of the Membership Committee by the Elections Committee prior to the Annual Meeting.

H. Official membership in the Association will be validated by members of the Elections Committee at the Annual Meeting. After the election, ballots will be tallied by members of the Election Committee and the results verified and reported to the membership by the end of the Annual Meeting.

I. If no member of the Elections Committee is present at the Annual Meeting, the President will designate other members present at the Annual Meeting to validate membership, accept proxies, and count ballots.

J. In the case of the tie vote, a run-off election will be held at the Annual Meeting to break the tie for that position. Proxy voters are not cast in run-off elections; only those members in actual attendance may vote to break the tie.

Article X Membership Year

The membership year shall correspond to the membership year of the National Association of Disability Examiners.

Article XI Formation of District Chapters

A. District Chapters may be formed in the geographic areas corresponding with the District Disability Offices.

B. District Chapters will draft a Constitution and Bylaws which is compatible with the Association’s Constitution and Bylaws.

C. District Chapters’ Constitution and Bylaws will be subject to approval of the Board of Directors with subsequent approval of the Association at the Annual Meeting.

D. State Conference profit, following return of seed money to the State Chapter, will be split, with the local host chapter retaining 50% of the profit and the State Chapter receiving 50% of the profit.

Article XII Awards/Recognition

1. The Association Awards Committee shall solicit nominations for the national awards, as well as awards recommended by the Board.
2. Certificates of recognition will be presented at the discretion of the Awards

and Recognition Committee.

Article XIII Dues

Dues to the National Association of Disability Examiners may be paid in equal installments over two consecutive months, if paid through the Association Treasurer prior to the annual renewal date. Guidelines for the payment plan will be established and administered by the Association Treasurer.

Article XIV Amendments

A. Amendments and revisions to this document may be made at the Annual Meeting.

B. Proposed amendments or revisions to this constitution must be presented in writing at the Annual Meeting, read by the Secretary, and necessary actions taken at that time. If 2/3 of the members present vote in favor of such amendment/revision, this constitution shall be so amended or revised.

Article XV Rules of Order

Robert’s Rules of Order-Revised shall govern meetings of the Executive Board and Annual Meetings when they are not in conflict with this constitution.
