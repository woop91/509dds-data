**CONSTITUTION**

**OF THE**

**TENNESSEE ASSOCIATION OF DISABILITY EXAMINERS, INC.**

**ARTICLE I – NAME**

The name of this organization shall be the Tennessee Association of Disability Examiners, Inc. (TADE).

**ARTICLE II- RELATIONSHIPS**

Section 1

The Tennessee Association of Disability Examiners is a State Chapter of the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

**ARTICLE III – DEFINITIONS**

For the purpose of this Constitution:

Section 1

Association means the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

Section 2

Chapter means the Tennessee Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

**ARTICLE IV – PURPOSES**

Section 1

Develop professional standards and training opportunities for all individuals engaged in the adjudication of disability claims.

Section 2

Promote the advancement of disability evaluation as a field of science and as a profession.

Section 3

Develop understanding of the mutual roles imposed on the several persons and professions involved in the process of evaluation of disability determinations.

**ARTICLE V – MEMBERSHIP**

Section 1

Members of the Chapter shall be members of the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc., and employed by or retired from a private or public agency, engaged in activities involved in the processing, documentation and evaluation of medical and vocational information submitted in support of applications for disability determination.

Section 2

Chapter dues shall be optional on the vote of the general membership.

Section 3

The membership year of the Chapter shall begin July 1 and end June 30.

**ARTICLE VI – MEETINGS**

Section 1

The official business meeting of the membership of the Chapter shall be held monthly. The President may call other meetings as may be necessary or additional meetings may be called upon petition of 25% of the membership.

Section 2

The Board of Directors shall meet at least once every two months.

Section 3

Members shall be notified of meetings by written announcement at least three work days prior to each meeting; notification shall include a proposed business agenda.

Section 4

Voting on business issues will be open unless a motion for a secret ballot is made and passed by a majority vote of those voting. All balloting for officers will be held by secret ballot and majority votes will carry all issues.

Section 5

Members desiring to take advantage of their proxy vote must identify their proxy to the secretary prior to the convening session.

**ARTICLE VII – ELECTION AND NOMINATIONS**

Section 1

The officers and board members of the Chapter shall be elected at the business meeting in August of each year with installation of the officers effective immediately following the election.

Section 2

A slate of officers and board members as proposed by the Nominations and Awards Committee shall be presented to the Chapter members by the President at the July business meeting. Other nominations may be presented from the floor at the August business meeting. All nominees must be members in good standing.

**ARTICLE VIII – OFFICERS**

The officers of the Chapter shall be: President, President-Elect, Secretary, Treasurer, and Parliamentarian. The officers shall be elected annually for terms of one year. Officers may be elected for not more than two consecutive terms to the same office and may be re-elected after one year. The Chapter will also elect a Regional Board Member, in even numbered years, to serve a 2-year term, with no term limitations. The duties of the officers shall be those usually pertaining to such positions including the following specific duties assigned to each office:

Section 1

The President shall preside at all meetings, appoint committees, call meetings, exercise supervision over the affairs of the Chapter, aid in coordinating the work of this Chapter with that of the Regional and National Chapters, and notify all members of meetings.

Section 2

The President-Elect shall preside in the absence of the President. In the event that the President is unable to complete the term of office, the President-Elect will assume the vacant position.

Section 3

The Secretary shall keep a record of all the minutes of the Chapter and of the Board of Directors and shall work with the TADE Corporate Officer to ensure annual reports are filed on a timely basis with the State of Tennessee.

Section 4

The Treasurer shall collect any fees or dues voted by the membership, have custody of the funds of the Chapter, make such expenditures as authorized by the Board of Directors, and keep a record of all funds received and expended.

Section 5

The Parliamentarian shall advise the officers and members of the chapter on questions of parliamentary procedures.

Section 6

The Regional Board Member will observe and advise the chapter to assure integrity and compliance with NADE. The Regional Board Member will seek the counsel of the designated NADE Southeast Regional Director when issues arise and report to the Board such findings. The Regional Board Member will also serve as a representative in promoting community understanding and awareness of the mission of NADE.

Section 7

Appointed Officers:

The President shall appoint chairpersons of committees to carry out the purposes of the chapter.

**ARTICE IX – BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

The Board of Directors shall consist of the elected officers of the Chapter; the immediate Past-President, and two members-at-large selected from the membership. The TADE Corporate Officer, the Director of DDS, and all Past-Presidents of TADE shall serve as ex officio Board Members, provided they are members of NADE. The Board of Directors shall:

Section 1

Have the authority to authorize expenditures of funds not to exceed 25% of the current fund balance, except for necessary supplies. Other expenditures must be approved by a majority vote of the monthly meetings.

Section 2

Make necessary arrangements for meetings.

Section 3

Meet at the call of the President or upon petition of a majority of the Board members.

Section 4

Have the power to authorize the President to appoint chairpersons of committees to carry out the purposes of the Chapter.

Section 5

Prior to each meeting prepare a business agenda, which would be submitted to the membership in conjunction with notification of meetings.

Section 6

Take such steps as may be necessary to carry out a program or function determined by vote of the Board members and for which there is no other provision.

**ARTICLE X – COMMITTEES**

There shall be four standing committees as follows: Constitution, By-Laws and Resolution Committee; Nominations and Awards Committee; Membership Committee; and Program Committee.

Section 1

The Constitution, By-Laws and Resolution Committee shall study the Constitution for possible revision or amendment, receive and study suggestions from the general membership for revision or amendments, and present written recommendations at the business session of the Chapter. This committee shall also originate, receive, and process resolutions for written presentation to the Board of Directors of the Chapter 30 days prior to business session.

Section 2

The Nominations and Awards Committee shall submit a prepared slate of nominees to the President at least 30 days prior to the date of the July business meeting. If feasible, the names of two members, who have agreed to serve, if elected, shall be presented for each office. This committee will also submit names and citations for awards at the regional and national level.

Section 3

The Membership Committee shall encourage interest in the Chapter and will secure the membership of eligible persons.

Section 4

The function of the Program Committee shall be to recommend to the Board of Directors a comprehensive program of activities for the Chapter, including the planning of the program for the annual meeting.

**ARTICLE XI – PARLIAMENTARY AUTHORITY**

The latest edition of Robert’s Rules of Order shall govern the Chapter in all cases provided that if the said “rules” are in conflict with the Constitution the latter shall govern.

**ARTICLE XII – BY-LAWS**

To implement this Constitution, the membership may adopt By-Laws, not inconsistent with this document by a majority vote of the members present at the July business meeting.

**ARTICLE XIII – AMENDMENT OF CONSTITUTION**

Section 1

The Constitution may be amended by a 2/3 vote of those members attending the Chapter meeting provided notices of business meeting and proposed constitutional changes have been sent to the members at least one week prior to the meeting, indicating that these amendments to the Constitution will be considered as part of the business of that meeting.

Section 2

Any amendment that is legally voted into action shall be effective immediately, unless otherwise designated.

Section 3

Any amendment or change to the NADE (National Association of Disability Examiners) Constitution which is in conflict with this TADE Constitution will require an amendment of the TADE Constitution to conform with the requirements of the NADE Constitution.

**OF THE**

**TENNESSEE ASSOCIATION OF DISABILITY EXAMINERS, INC.**

**ARTICLE I – BY-LAWS**

This document shall be known as the By-Laws of the Tennessee Association of Disability Examiners, Inc., adopted pursuant to Article XII of the Constitution of the Chapter.

**ARTICLE II - DEFINITIONS**

For the purpose of these By-Laws:

Section 1

Association means the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

Section 2

Southeast Region means those states comprising the Southeast Region as recognized by the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

Section 3

Chapter means the Tennessee Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

**ARTICLE III – MEMBERSHIP**

Section 1

There shall be two classes of membership in the Chapter (Professional, Associate) and membership is open to any interested individual who is also a member of the National Association of Disability Examiners and who is employed by or retired from a private or public agency engaged in activities involved in the documentation and/or evaluation of medical and/or vocational information submitted in support of applications for disability determination.

Section 2 - Dues

Dues in the Tennessee Association of Disability Examiners shall be optional on the vote of the general membership at the annual meeting.

Section 3 - Membership Year

The membership year shall begin July 1 and end June 30.

Section 4 - Privileges of Membership

All members shall be entitled to participate in the monthly meetings, any called meetings of the membership, and shall have full voting power.

**ARTICLE IV – ADMINISTRATION**

Section 1 - Officers

The Officers of the Chapter shall be a President, President-Elect, Secretary, Treasurer, Parliamentarian, and Regional Board Member. At the outset of organization of the State Chapter, the membership at the first annual meeting shall elect a President, Secretary, and Treasurer for the current year as well as a President-Elect, Secretary, and Treasurer for the coming year.

Section 2 - Term of Office

The President-Elect, Secretary, Treasurer, and Parliamentarian shall be elected annually for terms of one year. The Regional Board Member shall be elected in even numbered years for a term of two years.

Section 3 - Duties of Officers

The duties of the officers shall be those usually pertaining to such positions, including specific duties assigned to each officer specified in these By-Laws.

1. Duties of the President

The President’s duties shall be to preside at all meetings, appoint committees, call meetings, exercise supervision over the affairs of the Chapter, aid in coordinating the work of this Chapter with that of the NADE Regional and National Organization, and notify members of all meetings.

1. Duties of the President-Elect

The President-Elect shall preside in the absence of the President. In the event that the President is unable to complete the term of office, the President-Elect will assume the vacant position.

1. Duties of the Secretary

The Secretary shall keep a record of all the minutes of the Chapter and of the Board of Directors and shall work with the TADE Corporate Officer to ensure annual reports are filed on a timely basis with the State of Tennessee.

D. Duties of the Treasurer

The Treasurer shall collect any fees or dues voted by the membership, have custody of the funds of the Chapter, make such expenditures as is authorized by the Board of Directors, and keep a record of all money received and expended.

1. Duties of the Parliamentarian

The Parliamentarian shall advise the officers and members of the chapter on questions of parliamentary procedures.

1. Duties of the Regional Board Member

The Regional Board Member will observe and advise the chapter to assure integrity and compliance with NADE. The Regional Board Member will seek the counsel of the designated NADE Southeast Regional Director when issues arise and report to the Board such findings. The Regional Board Member will also serve as a representative in promoting community understanding and awareness of the mission of NADE.

Section 4 - Board of Directors

The Board of Directors shall consist of the elected officers of the Chapter; the immediate Past-President, and two members-at-large selected from the membership. The TADE Corporate Officer, the Director of DDS, and all Past-Presidents of TADE shall serve as ex officio Board Members, provided they are members of NADE.

Section 5 - Duties of the Board of Directors

The duties of the Board of Directors are those as outlined in the Constitution of this Chapter plus any additional duties as may be voted by majority decision of the membership at the annual meeting.

Section 6 - Committees

The standing committees of the Chapter shall be: (1) Constitution and By-Laws Committee, (2) Nominations and Awards Committee, (3) Membership Committee; and (4) Program Committee.

Section 7 - Appointment of Standing Committees

Not later than 60 days following the adjournment of the annual meeting of the Chapter, the President, subject to confirmation by the Board of Directors, shall appoint the members and name the Chairman of the Standing Committees.

Section 8 - Duties of the Standing Committees are:

1. Constitution and By-Laws Committee.

The function of this committee shall be to study the Constitution and By-Laws for possible revision or amendment, receive and study suggestions for revision and amendment, and present the recommendations to the membership at the annual meeting.

1. Nominations and Awards Committee.

This committee shall meet prior to the annual meeting of the Chapter, and shall nominate two, if possible, members for each office which shall become vacant at the time of the annual meeting, and report the nominations to the membership at the annual meeting. Nominations from the floor shall be allowed. The Nominations and Awards Committee shall also be responsible for nomination of members for all awards and honors as deemed appropriate.

1. Membership Committee.

The function of this committee shall be to recommend to the Board of Directors a comprehensive program of activities for the Chapter, including the planning of the program for the annual meeting.

1. Program Committee.

The function of this committee is to recommend to the Board of Directors a plan for each of the meetings of the membership, arrange for any speakers to come to the meetings, and to be responsible for a formal expression of appreciation to those speakers.

**ARTICLE V – PARLIAMENTARY AUTHORITY**

The latest edition of Robert’s Rules of Order shall govern the Chapter in all meetings provided that if the said rules are in conflict with the Constitution the latter shall govern.

**ARTICLE VI – AMENDMENTS**

The By-Laws of the Association may be amended as follows:

An amendment may be proposed by any member to the Board of Directors at its monthly meeting. If such a proposed amendment is approved by a majority of the Board of Directors, it shall be referred for study to the Constitution and By-Laws Committee. Upon receiving the report of the Constitution and By-Laws Committee, the Board of Directors shall present the proposed amendment to the membership at the next annual meeting. Amendments shall be effective upon ratification by 2/3 vote of members present.

**ARTICE VII – MICELLANEOUS**

Section 1

These By-Laws may be amended by a 2/3 vote of those members attending the Chapter meeting provided notices of business meeting and proposed constitutional changes have been sent to the members at least one week prior to the meeting, indicating that these amendments to the Constitution will be considered as part of the business of that meeting.

Section 2

Any amendment that is legally voted into action shall be effective immediately, unless otherwise designated.

Section 3

Any amendment or change to the NADE (National Association of Disability Examiners) By-Laws which is in conflict with this TADE By-Laws will require an amendment of the TADE By-Laws to conform to the requirements of the NADE By-Laws.

Section 4

Immediately following the ratification of the Constitution and By-Laws an election of officers shall be held.
