Research summary covering state Disability Determination Services examiner union coverage for CA, OR, AK, HI, NV, NM, CO, MT, ID, UT, WY. Excludes WA (WFSE GG; covered separately) and AZ (non-bargaining; covered separately).

## California (CA)

**DDS Administering Agency:**  
California Department of Social Services (CDSS), Disability Determination Services Division (DDSD)

**Examiner Classification:**  
Disability Evaluation Analyst (DEA) I, II, III

**Bargaining Unit:**  
Unit 4 (Professional, Administrative, and Technical)

**Union & Affiliate:**  
SEIU Local 1000 (Service Employees International Union, California State Employees)

**CBA Effective Dates:**  
2023–2026 (Tentative Agreement reached 2024; supersedes 2020–2023)

**Key Provisions:**  
- Joint Labor Management Committee (JLMC) established to address DDS workplace issues; meets semi-annually minimum
- Recruitment and Retention differential: $2,000–$3,000 annually for DEA and related classifications
- Largest three-year contract in Local 1000 history (as of 2024)

**Supervisor Unit Status:**  
SEIU Local 1000 Unit 11 (Supervisory)

**Sources:**

## Summary: Bargaining Landscape

### States with Confirmed/Likely Union Coverage:
- **CA**: ✓ Confirmed (SEIU Local 1000, Unit 4)
- **OR**: ✓ Likely (SEIU Local 503, Human Services Coalition or DAS)
- **AK**: ✓ Likely (ASEA/AFSCME Local 52, GGU)
- **HI**: ✓ Likely (HGEA/AFSCME Local 152, Unit 13)
- **NV**: ✓ Confirmed (AFSCME Local 4041, Unit C) — Post-AB 271 (2019) reform; recent formalization (2024)
- **NM**: ? Uncertain (CWA Local 7076 has broad coverage; DDS assignment not verified)
- **CO**: ? Uncertain (State bargaining authorized 2020; specific DDS agreement terms not publicly available)
- **MT**: ✓ Likely (MFPE, biennial bargaining; DDS presumed covered under DPHHS agreements)

### States with No/Limited Union Coverage:
- **ID**: ✗ No systematic state-level bargaining
- **UT**: ✗ Banned as of 2025 (referendum pending)
- **WY**: ✗ Right-to-work state; no systematic state-level bargaining

### Key Surprises & Findings:

1. **Nevada's Post-AB 271 Reform (2019)**: Nevada shifted from right-to-work to limited bargaining post-2019. DWSS/AFSCME Local 4041 Unit C formally won representation in August 2024 with 88% affirmation. This is one of the most recent state bargaining victories among Western peers.

2. **Colorado's 2020 Bargaining Authorization**: Colorado granted state employees collective bargaining rights via 2020 law, but specific CDHS/DDS contract terms remain opaque in public records. Colorado WINS operates under the new framework, but details require direct agency contact.

3. **Montana's Robust Biennial Bargaining**: Montana maintains 64 active collective bargaining agreements with regular economic and contract negotiations. DPHHS presumed to cover DDS examiners, but specific job classification not explicitly listed in available documents.

4. **Utah's Recent (2025) Bargaining Ban**: Utah legislature banned public sector collective bargaining in 2025, pending a 2026 referendum. This is the most recent change, reversing prior limited bargaining rights.

5. **Idaho & Wyoming Non-Bargaining**: Idaho and Wyoming do not systematically provide state-level collective bargaining for public employees, limiting union coverage.

### Recommended Follow-Up:

- **Colorado DDS**: Contact Colorado WINS or AFSCME Council 18 directly for confirmation of current bargaining unit assignment and active contract terms.
- **New Mexico DDS**: Verify which state agency houses NM DDS and confirm CWA Local 7076 covers that agency.
- **Idaho & Wyoming**: Confirm via state labor offices whether DDS examiners have any informal or alternative union representation.
- **Hawaii**: Monitor June 2025 contract expiration; new negotiations may alter Unit 13 terms.
