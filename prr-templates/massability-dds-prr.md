**Subject:** Public Records Request — Disability Determination Services (DDS)
division reports, budget, systems, telework, and workforce records,
FY2002–FY2026

**Body:**

Pursuant to the Massachusetts Public Records Law, M.G.L. c. 66, § 10, I request
the following records of the **Disability Determination Services (DDS) division**
of MassAbility (formerly the Massachusetts Rehabilitation Commission). Where a
fiscal-year range is given, please provide every year for which a responsive
record exists, even if some years are missing.

### Section 1 — Annual / Section 9F reports

1. The complete Annual Report and/or Section 9F report submitted by the DDS
   division to the Legislature for each fiscal year **2002 through 2022** (the
   years not currently published on mass.gov).

### Section 2 — Budget, by fiscal year (FY2013–FY2022)

2. The DDS division's annual operating budget (the federal SSA reimbursement
   received), for each fiscal year 2013–2022.
3. Any available breakdown of that budget by category (personnel, medical/
   psychological consultants, consultative-exam purchases, IT/systems, fixed
   costs).

### Section 3 — System & operational defects / COVID remote operations

4. Records sufficient to show the **incoming-fax failure rate** for DDS fax
   numbers, including: IT/helpdesk tickets, vendor or SSA/NCPS correspondence
   about fax-delivery failures or **false delivery acknowledgments** (where the
   system reports a fax received that was in fact dropped), and any analysis of
   fax reliability, **2023–present**. Also any records describing the process by
   which DDS can request that an NCPS letter template be edited to reflect an
   updated fax number.
5. **Case-processing system availability** records (DCPS / eCAT / MCS or the
   successor system): outage logs, scheduled- and unplanned-downtime records,
   and helpdesk ticket volumes affecting DDS, **2023–present**.
6. **Consultative-exam scheduling performance:** records sufficient to show CE
   no-show rates, cancellation rates, and vendor turnaround time (days from CE
   order to report received), **2020–present**.
7. **Medical-evidence turnaround:** records sufficient to show average days for
   providers to return requested MER and the count of outstanding/overdue MER
   requests, **2020–present**.
8. **Decision-notice mailing:** records sufficient to show outgoing
   decision-notice print/mail delays and returned-mail rates, **2020–present**.
9. Records sufficient to show the date on which MassAbility/MRC DDS employees
   were first directed, authorized, or required to work from home or telework
   because of COVID-19, and whether that initial virtual-work arrangement was
   full-time / five days per week or included any required in-office rotation,
   including March 2020 emails, memoranda, telework directives,
   remote-access/VPN rollout notices, equipment pickup instructions, Remote
   Access Survey materials, staffing/coverage schedules, and return-to-office
   plans.
10. Records sufficient to show each later change in DDS telework, virtual-work,
    hybrid-work, or return-to-office schedule from March 2020 to present,
    including records showing when DDS employees transitioned from full-time or
    five-day virtual work to a three-day schedule. This request specifically
    seeks records reflecting, confirming, correcting, or disputing the working
    note that DDS transitioned to a three-day schedule during the last week of
    January; if the year or exact date differs, please identify the correct
    effective date and schedule.

### Section 4 — Workforce

11. Records sufficient to show, by month or by year, the count of **authorized
    but unfilled** Vocational Disability Examiner positions, the **average
    time-to-fill**, and the count of **separations** with reason code
    (resignation / retirement / termination / transfer), **2015–present**.
12. Records sufficient to show **examiner-training** class sizes, trainee
    attrition, and time-to-certification (time for a new examiner to reach full
    casework), **2015–present**.
13. The **Form 30 position descriptions** for the Vocational Disability Examiner
    series (all grades) and for DDS first-line supervisors.
14. **Aggregate** counts (no PII) of **grievances** filed and their dispositions,
    and of **reasonable-accommodation** requests and their outcomes/average
    processing time, within the DDS division, **2018–present**.
15. Records sufficient to identify whether MassAbility/MRC or DDS conducted,
    commissioned, received, or presented any DDS-specific or DDS-inclusive
    employee/staff climate, culture, morale, inclusion, equity, workplace,
    engagement, or organizational-assessment survey/report during **2017–2020**,
    including any survey reportedly conducted by an outside firm one or two
    years before the COVID-19 pandemic and any MRC internal staff climate survey
    related to LGBTQ issues. Please include the vendor/firm name (if any),
    contract, statement of work, purchase order, survey instrument, aggregate
    results, report, executive summary, presentation deck, recommendations,
    follow-up action plan, and transmittal communications. Aggregate records are
    requested; no individual respondent identities are sought.

## Public-interest note (voluntary)

These records document workforce, budget, systems, telework, and
workplace-climate records at a DDS division that MassAbility's own FY2025
reporting describes as facing unprecedented staffing vacancies. Public visibility
into how staffing, budget, system reliability, remote-work policy, and
workplace-climate records interact has direct relevance to claimants' wait times
and to the public understanding of how the federal-state DDS partnership
operates in Massachusetts. The underlying open dataset is at
github.com/Woop91/509dds-data.
