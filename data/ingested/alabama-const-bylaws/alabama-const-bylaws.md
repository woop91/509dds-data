CONSTITUTION

OF THE

Alabama Association of Disability Examiners

ARTICLE I

NAME

This professional, fraternal, non-profit organization shall be named the Alabama Association of Disability Examiners (AADE) and is officially affiliated with the National Association of Disability Examiners (NADE).

ARTICLE II

RELATIONSHIP TO NADE

The Alabama Association of Disability Examiners is a professional chapter of the National Association of Disability Examiners (NADE) and, as such, is subject to the provisions of the Constitution and By-laws of NADE.

ARITCLE III

DEFINITIONS

For the purpose of this Constitution, and the accompanying By-laws:

Association means the National Association of Disability Examiners, also known as NADE.

Board or Executive Board means the Board of Directors of the Alabama Association of Disability Examiners (AADE).

Chapter means a unit organized at a local level to carry out the purposes of the National Association of Disability Examiners (NADE).

Region means any one of the geographical areas of the Association which shall be established by the Board of Directors of NADE.

Southeast Region means the particular region of which AADE is a member. This region was created by the Board of Directors of NADE and its member states (chapters) include: Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina and Tennessee.

ARTICLE IV

PURPOSES

The purposes for which this organization has been established are:

1. To increase public understanding of all disability benefit programs.
2. To develop professional standards and training opportunities for all individuals engaged in the adjudication of disability claims.
3. To improve the documentation of applications for disability benefits and the evaluation of medical and vocational information obtained in connection with such applications.
4. To provide a forum for the discussion of problems related to the adjudication of disability claims.

ARTICLE V

MEMBERSHIP

Members of AADE must be in good standing of the National Association of Disability Examiners. This shall include those persons involved in the administration of a disability program and those persons involved in research, training, or study of the disability process.

Membership of AADE shall be available in two classes, full and associate. The rights and privileges of each class of membership is designated by Article III of the By-laws of NADE.

ARTICLE VI

LIMITATIONS

AADE shall not participate in any activity not permitted for a tax-exempt, non-profit organization. This shall include, but is not limited to, the participation in political campaigns on behalf of any individual seeking election to public office.

In addition, no profit or asset of AADE shall be given to any individual member with the exception that the AADE Board of Directors may authorize partial or full reimbursement of a member’s expenses when those expenses were incurred while acting in an official capacity on behalf of AADE, provided that such actions has previously been approved by AADE’s Board of Directors. Should AADE dissolve, all monies and assets of the organization will be distributed in a manner directed by the legal requirements of tax-exempt organizations.

ARTICLE VII

OFFICERS

The officers of AADE shall be a President, President Elect, Secretary and Treasurer. The President, President Elect, Secretary and Treasurer shall be elected for a term of office of one year and each of these officers shall be limited to serving only two consecutive terms in a particular office. A Regional Board Member shall be elected for a term of two years and shall be limited to serving only two consecutive terms. The President, President Elect, Secretary, Treasurer and Regional Board Member of AADE shall each have been a member of AADE for at least one year, which immediately precedes the date they assume office.

The duties of each officer of AADE shall be specified in the By-Laws of the organization.

ARTICLE VIII

BOARD OF DIRECTCORS

The Board of Directors of AADE shall consist of the four elected officers of the Chapter, the immediate Past President of AADE and three directors elected at-large and the Regional Board Member. Each of the at-large directors shall be elected for a term of office of one year and shall be limited to serving two consecutive terms. Directors shall have been a member of AADE for one full year prior to their being elected to serve.

The duties of the Board of Directors shall be specified in the By-Laws of the organization.

ARTICLE IX

MEETINGS

There shall be quarterly meetings of the Chapter each fiscal year at a time and place designated by the President of the Chapter. The President may call other meetings of the Chapter as necessary. The purpose of all meetings shall be clearly stated in the public notice of the meeting distributed by the Secretary of the Chapter. The President Elect shall assist the President in the specifics of the meeting, ie, place and time to be held at the President’s discretion.

ARTICLE X

BY – LAWS

To implement this Constitution, the Chapter shall adopt By-Laws consistent with the Constitution of AADE and do not conflict with the Constitution and By-Laws of NADE.

ARTICLE XI

AMENDMENTS

The Constitution of the Alabama Association of Disability Examiners may be amended by two-thirds majority vote of those members in attendance at any business meeting of the Chapter, provided that such proposed amendments were distributed to the members for their review at least two days in advance of the meeting.

BY – LAWS

OF THE

ALABAMA ASSOCIATION OF DISABILITY EXAMINERS

**ARTICLE I**

**DOCUMENT NAME AND PURPOSE**

This document shall be known as the By-laws of the Alabama Association of Disability Examiners (AADE). The purpose of these By-laws is to implement the Constitution of AADE.

**ARTICLE II**

**ELECTION OF OFFICERS**

Election of officers and directors of AADE shall take place in the following prescribed manner:

1. The date of election shall be the second Thursday in July. In the event that the election cannot be held on this date, the Elections Committee shall have the authority to designate the date of election, provided that the date set by the Elections Committee shall not be before the third Thursday and no later than July 31.
2. The President of AADE shall appoint the Election Committee to conduct the election. This committee shall have at least three members, none of whom shall be entitled to become a candidate in the election. The immediate past president of AADE shall be appointed to serve as the chairperson of the Elections Committee. In the event that this is not possible, the President of AADE shall designate another AADE member as chairperson of this committee. The Elections Committee shall be appointed at least thirty days before the date of the elections.
3. The Elections Committee shall certify those members eligible to vote and shall monitor the election and certify the election results. Absentee ballot voting will be permitted. Members voting in this manner should obtain a ballot from the chair person of the Elections Committee and return the ballot before election day. Candidates shall be permitted to be present when votes are counted if they so desire.
4. In the event that no candidate for a particular office receives a majority of the votes cast for that office, a runoff election shall take place three working days after the primary election. The runoff election ballot shall have only the names of the two candidates who received the most votes in the primary election.
5. AADE members who desire to have their names on the primary election ballot should notify the Chairperson of the Elections Committee at least ten days prior to election day. The Election Committee shall certify the membership credentials of all candidates.
6. The terms of office for AADE officers shall begin on August 1 and shall conclude on July 31 of the following year.

**ARTICLE III**

**DUTIES OF AADE OFFICERS**

The duties of the officers of AADE shall be those duties which usually pertain to such positions, and shall also include the following specific duties:

President – shall preside at all meetings of the Chapter, including meetings of the Board of Directors of AADE; shall appoint standing committees authorized in these By-Laws, and the chairpersons of these committees; shall appoint any other committees and their chairpersons as may be deemed necessary by the President or Executive Board; shall exercise supervision over all business matters involving AADE and aid in coordinating the work of AADE with that of its parent organization (NADE). Should the need arise for a general budget, it will be the responsibility of the President to develop and submit same to the Board of Directors for an approval. The President shall call meetings of the Board of Directors as the need may appear, or upon the written request of two members of the AADE Board of Directors.

President Elect – shall preside at meetings of the Chapter and the Board of Directors in the absence of the President. The President Elect may be assigned other duties by the President or Board of Directors of AADE.

Secretary - shall record the minutes of all meetings of the Chapter, including meetings of the Board of Directors of AADE; shall notify all members of AADE of the date, time and location of AADE meetings. Such notification shall occur at least three days in advance of the meeting. The Secretary shall keep a ledger of all income and expenditures of AADE as presented by the treasurer of AADE so as to verify the accuracy. The Secretary may also be assigned other duties by the President or Board of Director of AADE.

Treasurer – shall have custody of all monies belonging to AADE and shall

disburse the same pursuant to the orders of the Board of Directors of AADE or the general membership of the organization. The Treasurer shall distribute written financial reports at all meetings of the Board of Directors and at all business meetings of the Chapter. These reports shall be current and specific with regards to receipts and disbursements of AADE funds. All monies received or disbursed will be recorded and forwarded to the Secretary for verification; a;; deposits will be verified by the President or Secretary. The Treasurer may also be assigned other duties by the President or Board of Directors of AADE.

Regional Board Member – shall be the Chapter Representative on the Southeast

Regional Board and shall be a voting member of the Chapter Board of Directors. The Regional Board member shall represent the Chapter at official functions of NADE in the event the President is unable to carry out these duties. The Regional Board member may also be assigned other duties by the President or Board of Directors of AADE.

**ARTICLE IV**

**DUTIES OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

The Board of Directors of AADE shall conduct the business of the Chapter. It shall be considered that a quorum is present for the transaction of business when four members of the Board have been recorded as present. The Board of Directors of AADE shall have the following specific duties:

1. The Board shall have the responsibility to authorize expenditure of

AADE funds as need is determined. The Board shall have the

responsibility to insure sufficient funds for any expenditure.

2. The Board shall have the authority to fill vacancies within its own body.

Should any position on the Board of Directors become vacant, other than

the immediate past president, the remaining members of the Board shall

elect, by majority vote, an AADE member to complete the term of office of

the position which was vacated.

1. The Board shall take such steps as may be appropriate to carry out a

program or function previously authorized by the general membership of the Chapter and which was not otherwise provided for.

**ARTICLE V**

**FISCAL YEAR**

The fiscal year of AADE shall begin on August 1 and conclude on July 31 of the following year, coinciding with the terms of office of AADE’s elected officers. The Treasurer of AADE who served during the fiscal year shall make available to the general membership a written report of AADE’s finances, including a detailed account of all receipts and disbursements which occurred during the fiscal year. This report shall be made available within ten (10) days following the conclusion of the fiscal year. The President may appoint three members of the Chapter to conduct an internal audit. Members appointed to conduct such an audit shall not have served on the Board of Directors of AADE during the fiscal year for which the audit is being conducted. The President shall appoint on of the three members as chairperson of the audit committee. The committee shall report their findings to the Board of Directors.

**ARTICLE VI**

**STANDING COMMITTEES**

There shall be eight standing committees of AADE, through which the work of the organization will be accomplished. The President of AADE shall appoint the members and the chairpersons of each committee for one year terms to coincide with the President’s term of office. Committee chairpersons will be eligible for reappointment for three consecutive terms. Each committee should be represented at AADE Board meetings. The five standing committees of AADE are:

1. Constitution and By-laws Committee. This committee shall study the

constitution and By- laws of AADE for possible revision, to receive and

process proposed amendments to the Constitution and/or By-laws and present

the committee’s recommendations to the general membership for definitive

action. This committee shall also be charged with the responsibility to review

any resolutions which the Chapter, or any member thereof, may wish to

submit for consideration at regional and/or national conferences sponsored by

NADE.

1. Ways and Means Committee. This committee shall develop ideas and

formulate methods to raise the funds necessary for AADE to operate during

the fiscal year.

1. Program and Conference Committee. This committee shall plan and arrange

special programs, seminars, and other such events to be sponsored by AADE.

1. Membership Committee. This committee shall develop ideas and formulate

methods for recruiting qualified persons for membership in AADE.

1. Social Committee. This committee shall make necessary arrangements for all

social activities sponsored by AADE and develop new ideas to promote

greater interaction among AADE members.

**ARTICLE VII**

**REGIONAL REPRESENTATION**

The Alabama Association of Disability Examiners shall be represented at official functions of the Southeast Region of the National Association of Disability Examiners by its President and its Regional Board Member. Both of these officers will have the authority to vote as members of the Board of Directors of the Southeast Region. In the event the President or Regional Board Member is unable to carry out these duties, the Chapter’s Board of Directors shall be empowered to designate a substitute.

**ARTICLE VIII**

**REPRESENTATION AT REGIONAL AND NATIONAL CONFERENCES**

The number of delegates chosen to attend the regional and national conferences sponsored by NADE is determined by state funding and state authorization. The order of priority for selecting delegates to attend these conferences shall be:

TRACK I – OFFICERS

The President of AADE, by virtue of the office held, shall attend all

national and regional conferences as the official representative of AADE.

If the President is unable to attend, the Board of Directors shall appoint an

appropriate representative.

TRACK II – ROSTER

A current rotation roster of all in-house members is to be kept by the

Secretary. The list is established by a drawing of names and added to

when a new member joins AADE. The new member shall be placed at the

end of the roster when the member joins AADE. A separate roster is kept

for regional and national conferences. Each person at the top of the roster

has the option to accept a delegate position or reject it. If it is rejected, the

member’s name goes to the end of the roster. If the delegate position is

accepted, the member shall be funded to the conference and the member’s

name will then be placed at the end of the roster. The rotation roster

ensures that each member shall have an opportunity to serve as a funded

delegate to a conference. Any member that is not located at the DDS will

be placed on the rotation roster at their request only.

**ARTICLE IX**

**PARLIAMENTARY AUTHORITY**

Robert’s Rules of Order shall serve as the standard parliamentary authority on all questions of parliamentary law and proceedings which may arise during AADE meetings. This authority shall govern in all cases to which the rules therein are applicable, and in which these rules are not inconsistent with the Constitution or By-laws or AADE.

**ARTICLE X**

**AMENDMENT OF BY-LAWS**

These By-Laws of the Alabama Association of Disability Examiners may be amended by a simple majority vote of those members in attendance at any business meeting of the Chapter, provided that such proposed amendments were distributed to membership for their review at least two days in advance of the meeting.
