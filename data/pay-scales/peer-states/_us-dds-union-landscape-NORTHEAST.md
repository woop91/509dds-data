## Key Findings & Gaps

**Confirmed Coverage (3 states):**
- **New York (PEF):** Strong documentation; PS&T Unit 5 clearly covers professional examiner roles at OTDA.
- **Connecticut (AFSCME Local 714):** P-2 unit explicitly includes social service professionals; SEBAC agreement spans 10 years (2017–2027).
- **Pennsylvania (SEIU 668):** Covers human services workers; likely includes DDS examiners, though not explicitly named in sources.

**Likely Coverage (3 states):**
- **Vermont (VSEA):** ~6,000 state employees represented; DDS examiners likely in Non-Management Unit.
- **Maine (MSEA-SEIU):** ~10,000 state workers in four units; DDS examiners likely in Professional-Technical or Administrative unit.
- **New Jersey (CWA / IFPTE):** Executive Branch CBA covers 35,000 state workers; DDS examiners likely under CWA Local 1036, but confirmation needed.

**Uncertain Coverage (4 states):**
- **Rhode Island (NEARI Council 94):** Small state system; no direct evidence of examiner union affiliation.
- **New Hampshire (SEA-NH):** 14 certified bargaining units; DDS examiners classification unknown.
- **Delaware (AFSCME Council 81 / CWA):** Limited public sector bargaining; specific DDS affiliation not documented.
- **District of Columbia (AFGE):** Complexity of federal SSA vs. DC government jurisdiction; unclear which AFGE bargaining unit (if any) covers local DDS examiners.

**Notable Pattern:**
All states with confirmed/likely union coverage align with AFL-CIO, SEIU, AFSCME, or CWA — all major public employee unions. Smaller Northeast states (RI, NH, VT) show less transparent bargaining documentation, suggesting either smaller scale operations or state-specific labor law limitations (especially Delaware, which has historically restricted public sector bargaining).

## Recommendations for Further Verification

1. **Direct Union Contact:** Contact each state's primary public employee union (PEF, AFSCME, SEIU, CWA, NEARI, VSEA, MSEA-SEIU, SEA-NH, AFGE) with specific state DDS agency and examiner classification to confirm bargaining unit assignment.

2. **State Labor/PERB Records:** Review state-specific Public Employment Relations Board (PERB) or labor relations authority records, which typically publish certified bargaining units and incumbent representatives.

3. **Job Classification Databases:** Access state personnel/civil service job classification systems (e.g., NY Civil Service Commission, PA Department of Human Resources, CT Department of Administrative Services) to identify the precise classification code for disability determination examiners and their assigned bargaining unit.

4. **Agency HR Departments:** Contact each state DDS agency's human resources office directly with the examiner job title and request confirmation of union representation and CBA coverage.

5. **CBA Documents:** Obtain and review full collective bargaining agreements (available on most state labor relations websites) to locate specific job titles or classification ranges covered under each unit's scope.
