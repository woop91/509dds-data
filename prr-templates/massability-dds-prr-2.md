**Subject:** Public Records Request — Disability Determination Services (DDS)
division operational metrics, workload, overtime, and special-population records,
FY2010–FY2025

**Body:**

Pursuant to the Massachusetts Public Records Law, M.G.L. c. 66, § 10, I request
the following records of the **Disability Determination Services (DDS) division**
of MassAbility (formerly the Massachusetts Rehabilitation Commission). Where a
fiscal-year range is given, please provide every year for which a responsive
record exists, even if some years are missing.

### Section 1 — Operational metrics, by fiscal year (FY2013–FY2025)

For each fiscal year, the DDS division's figures for:

1. Total claim receipts (initial + reconsideration + CDR + hearings)
2. Total claim dispositions (same scope)
3. Continuing Disability Review (CDR) receipts and dispositions
4. Consultative examinations (CE) purchased, and the CE rate as published
5. Medical Evidence of Record (MER) requests purchased, and the MER rate as published
6. Cost per case (per-disposition cost), with the calculation method used

*Note: I am specifically missing FY2021 and FY2022 for items 1–5, and the FY2025
figures once compiled. If those years are easiest to produce from the underlying
Section 9F worksheets, that is acceptable.*

### Section 2 — Two specific reconciliations

7. The **FY2022 favorable-determination ("% allowed") rate** as stated in the
   DDS Section 9F report, **and the underlying allowed-count and total-count**
   used to compute it. (A published MA figure of 57% diverges from SSA's FY2022
   figure of 46.76%; I am trying to reconcile the two.)
8. For the **homeless / unhoused population**, the **count** of disability claims
   processed for individuals identified as homeless or residing in a shelter, for
   each fiscal year **2013–2025**, together with the definition/method used to
   identify them. If only a rate is maintained (e.g., the 22% figure in the
   FY2023 report), please provide the **numerator and denominator** behind that
   rate. *(I am seeking a count, not a rate.)*

### Section 3 — Caseload & assignment (the workload record)

9. All **directives, memoranda, or policies** setting a weekly **case-assignment
   cap or intake target** for examiners (including any 18-case and any 20-case
   figure), and any record describing the **"case weight" methodology** used to
   count assignments, **2023–present**.
10. Any **productivity-expectation** memoranda or standards issued to examiners,
    **2023–present**.
11. Records sufficient to show the **distribution of pending caseload per
    examiner** and the **count of aged cases** (e.g., pending beyond a defined
    threshold), by office (Boston, Worcester), **2023–present**. *(Aggregate or
    de-identified figures are acceptable; no individual claimant or employee
    identifiers are sought.)*

### Section 4 — Overtime

12. **Overtime authorization** records for DDS — the memoranda authorizing
    overtime, the stated justification, and any dollar or hour caps — by pay
    period or by authorization event, **2010–present**.
13. Records sufficient to show **overtime hours offered and overtime hours used**
    (or overtime expenditures) for the DDS division, annually, **2010–present**.

### Section 5 — Special populations

14. Any **other special-population breakouts** (e.g., HIV/AIDS, veterans,
    compassionate-allowance, terminal-illness cases) disclosed in or compiled for
    DDS annual or Section 9F reporting, **2013–present**.

## Public-interest note (voluntary)

These records document operational metrics, workload, overtime, and
special-population reporting at a DDS division that MassAbility's own FY2025
reporting describes as facing unprecedented staffing vacancies. Public visibility
into these records has direct relevance to claimants' wait times and to the
public understanding of how the federal-state DDS partnership operates in
Massachusetts. The underlying open dataset is at github.com/Woop91/509dds-data.
