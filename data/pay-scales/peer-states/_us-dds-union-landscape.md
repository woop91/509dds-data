# US DDS Examiner Union Representation — National Landscape

> Which state Disability Determination Services (DDS) examiner workforces are
> covered by collective bargaining, and by which union/local. Compiled
> 2026-05-08 from regional research files (see "Regional source files"
> below). MA, MD, VA, WA, AZ, IN, MO, TN, WI come from the existing
> peer-states research; the rest from a parallel three-agent regional sweep.

## Why this file exists

The original peer-state salary work used a **population peer set** (states
~6-8M like MA). That's right for workload comparisons but wrong for
bargaining-table comparisons, where the apples-to-apples peers are *other
states whose DDS examiners have a CBA*. This file establishes that bargaining
peer set.

## Bargaining peer-set candidate

For an MA salary-comparison brief, the most directly comparable peers are
states with **confirmed DDS examiner CBAs** and a published, machine-readable
pay schedule. From the table above:

| Tier | States | Why |
|---|---|---|
| A — strongest peers (confirmed CBA + extracted pay grid) | **MD, WA** | Both have AFSCME or AFSCME-affiliated representation, full step grids extracted, and supervisors clearly classified |
| B — confirmed CBA, pay-data work needed | **NY, CT, PA, MN, CA, NV** | High union density, transparent agencies, but pay schedules not yet pulled in this repo |
| C — likely CBA, needs verification before claims | **NJ, IL, MI, OR, AK, HI, MT, ME, VT** | Likely peers but specific DDS classification within the CBA needs confirmation |
| D — exclude from bargaining peer set | **AZ, VA, TN, IN, WI, MO + the non-bargaining South** | Different labor regime entirely; these are the *contrast* states, not peer states |

If you wanted to expand the salary-comparison work in priority order:
**NY → CT → PA → CA → MN** (tier B). All have transparent state HR and
public CBAs; the next-session pull would mirror the existing peer-states
folder structure for each.

## Key surprises

1. **The bargaining peer set is much smaller than expected.** Roughly 15
   states have confirmed or likely DDS examiner CBAs. The other 35 states are
   either right-to-work, have no certified state-employee bargaining, or had
   their bargaining structurally weakened (WI Act 10 in 2011, IN executive
   order in 2005, IA Act 97 in 2017, SD's 2018 law, UT's 2025 ban).
2. **Nevada is the newest entrant** — AFSCME Local 4041 formalized DDS
   bargaining in August 2024 after AB 271 (2019) opened the door. Useful
   recent precedent for organizing arguments.
3. **MAPE in Minnesota** is independent (not AFSCME-affiliated) — Minnesota
   Disability Examiners are class code 0871, Unit 214. Worth flagging when
   peer comparisons claim "all unionized DDS workforces are AFSCME or SEIU."
4. **The Virginia case is interesting** — VA repealed its prohibition on
   *local* public-sector bargaining in 2021 but state employees (including
   DDS Analysts) still have no certified bargaining. So VA shows up as a
   no-bargaining peer despite the recent reform.
5. **Maryland's supervisor unit (Unit S)** is structurally unusual: a
   *separate* AFSCME unit just for supervisors. Worth comparing to MA where
   Chief VDE / VDE IV appears to be in the same Unit 8 as line examiners.

## Caveats & follow-up

- "Likely" rows still need direct CBA-text verification. Specific class titles

  CBA's public summary.
- The peer-states-pay-summary.json salary data is currently population-peer
  scoped; expanding to bargaining-peer scope (Tier B states above) is the
  natural next data pull.
