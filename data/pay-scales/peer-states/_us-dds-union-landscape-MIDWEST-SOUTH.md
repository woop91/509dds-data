## SUMMARY OF FINDINGS

### States with Confirmed Union Representation (Midwest):
1. **Minnesota (MAPE)** — Confirmed, independent union, ~18,000 members, strong coverage
2. **Illinois (AFSCME Council 31)** — Likely confirmed, ~90,000 state employees, strong presence
3. **Michigan (SEIU Local 517M)** — Likely confirmed, ~7,500 members, HSS unit scope

### States with Limited or No Bargaining:
- **Iowa**: Severely restricted post-2017 Act 97 (wages + benefits only)
- **North Dakota, South Dakota**: ND allows bargaining; SD severely restricted (2018 law)
- **Nebraska, Kansas**: Enabling statutes exist but limited union presence confirmed
- **Kentucky, West Virginia**: No statutory framework; minimal/no bargaining

### Southern States (All Non-Bargaining):
- **Right-to-Work + No Bargaining**: TX, OK, AR, LA, MS, AL, NC, SC, GA
- **Permits Bargaining but Minimal Coverage**: FL (statute present; DDS unit unclear)

### Surprises & Gaps:
1. **Iowa bargaining gutted**: Expected AFSCME Council 61 strength; post-2017 reforms eliminated meaningful bargaining scope.
2. **Florida's exception**: Florida is the only Southern state with explicit bargaining rights, yet search results show minimal confirmable coverage for DDS.
3. **South Dakota's pivot**: Swing from permissive to restrictive (2018 law) eliminated meaningful collective bargaining.
4. **Kansas unclear**: Statute permits bargaining, but union presence is minimal in search results.
5. **Ohio/OCSEA assumption**: While OCSEA/AFSCME Local 11 covers ~35,000 state employees, no explicit DDS examiner confirmation found.

### Recommended Follow-Up:
- **Direct agency contact** for Iowa (verify post-Act 97 scope), Ohio (OCSEA), Florida (state LR office)
- **MAPE, AFSCME Council 31, SEIU 517M** for job classification specs confirming DDS examiner inclusion
- **Right-to-work states**: Confirm any voluntary union presence or professional associations representing DDS examiners
