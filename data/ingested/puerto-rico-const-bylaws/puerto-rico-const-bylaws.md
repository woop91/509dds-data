***PREAMBLE***

United by this Constitution, we, the members of the Puerto Rico Association of Disability

Examiners, promise to promote a high standard of professionalism and develop our

expertise in the adjudication of disability claims. It is our main concern to serve with

honesty arid integrity the people who rely on us for the review of their claims; to

encourage and enhance public knowledge about the service we offer to the claimants of

our community; and to ensure trust toward our program as a whole.

Article I

NAME AND AFFILIATION

Section 1: The name of this chapter shall be the Puerto Rico Association of

Disability Examiners.

Section 2: This chapter shall be an affiliate of the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc. As such, it shall conform to the constitutions and By-Laws of the National Association of Disability Examiners, Inc.

Article II

Purpose

It shall be the basic purpose of this organization to advance the adjudicative process in disability determinations as a multi-disciplinary science and as a profession and to strive for continuing improvement in the quality of services rendered by disability examiners to all physically and mentally disabled persons, with this goal in mind, we must:

Section 1: Provide opportunities for conferences and forms for the discussions of problems and legal regulations and standards to provide the best quality of services and the exchange of ideas between our divisions and all other concerned people.

Section 2: Provide a means of communication between all persons serving the disabled and the community so as to increase the public understanding of the disability program.

Section 3: Encourage full support to all private and public agencies engaged in helping the disabled.

Section 4: Stimulate professional training, research and participation in special programs which will advance the knowledge and skills of the disability examiner.

Section 5: Promote better fellowship among disability examiners and others involved in the program by providing an informal setting for social activities.

**By-Laws**

**Article I**

**By-Laws**

This document shall be known as the By-Laws of the Puerto Rico

Association of Disability Examiners, adapted pursuant to Article

VII of their Constitution.

**Article II**

**Definitions**

For the purpose of these By-Laws.

1. Disability Examiner means an employee of a disability program having the responsibility (acting independently or as a member of a team, board or panel) for a determining whether disability as defined by his/her agency is established by the evidence presented. A Disability Examiner maybe responsible for obtaining such evidence, interviewing applicants, or other auxiliary activities, with the decision making responsibility being the essential characteristics in determining whether a particular employee is a Disability Examiner. Any person trained and experienced in the making of such a determination and who is presently acting in a consulting capacity, even if in a restricted aspect of the decision making process) shall be considered a Disability Examiner.

**Article III**

**Classes of Membership**

Section 1: Full Members.

a) Any Disability Examiner as defined in Article TI of the By-Laws.

b) Any person reviewing disability determinations, with authority to revise or recommend revision of the disability decision.

c) Any person who Supervises disability examiners or who administers any agency whose principal function is the rendering or substantive review of disability determinations.

d) Any person serving as a member of the faculty or any college or university department or program providing specific education in the field of disability determination.

e) A medical or vocational professional who performs consulting medical or vocational services for a disability program.

f) Any retired person who at the time of retirement was a Full Member in

good standing

g) Any person not in the preceding categories, but who was a Full Member in good standing as of June 30. 1997 and who wishes to retain the classification of Full Member.

Section 2: Associate Members.

An Associate member shall be any person having special interest in the evaluation of claims for disability benefits, but who is not eligible for Full Membership.

**Article IV**

**Amendments to the By-Laws**

Section 1: Amendments. Suggestions for the amending of By-Laws may be received and presented to the By-Laws committee at a regular business meeting and should be considered within a period of ten (10) days. The By-Laws may be amended by a majority vote of the Board of Directors after due presentation to these by the By-Laws Committee.
