**PREAMBLE**

We, the members of the Jersey State Association of Disability Examiners

unite under this Constitution in order to promote a high Standard of

professionalism and develop our expertise in disability adjudication to its

full capacity. We desire to develop the .public trust in our integrity and

judgment by rendering the best possible service to the disabled c1aimants of

the State of New Jersey.

**CONSTITUTION**

**OF**

**JERSEY ASSOCIATTON OF DISABILITY EXAMINERS (JADE)**

**ARTICLE I: NAME**

The name of this organization shall be the Jersey Association of Disability Examiners, a professional chapter of the National Association of Disability Examiners and subject to the constitution and by-laws of NADE in any areas not covered here-in.

**ARTICLE II: PURPOSE**

To foster, promote, and participate in activities designed to:

1. Increase public understanding of disability benefit program.

2. Develop professional standards and training opportunities for all individuals engaged in adjudication of disability claims.

3. Improve the documentation from the medical community of applications for disability benefits to improve the evaluation of medical and

vocational information obtained in connection with such applications.

4 Provide a forum for the discussion of problems related to adjudication

of disability claims.

**ARTICLE III: COMPOSITION OF THE JERSEY CHAPTER**

All persons working in New Jersey in any way involved in the administration of, teaching,, training, or processing of the disability benefits program under the Social Security Act; shall be eligible for the chapter and NADE membership.

**ARTICLE IV: MEMBERSHIP**

SECTION 1: The members of the Jersey Association of Disability Examiners working in

the State of New Jersey shall constitute the membership of the chapter.

SECTION 2: There shall be no fees, registration or otherwise, unless found necessary

and so ordered by the Executive Board.

**ARTICLE V: EXECUTIVE BOARD**

SECTION 1: The Executive Board shall, consist of President, Vice President, Secretary,

Treasurer, Member-at-Large to be elected by membership. Appointed

Committee Chairperson shall attend the Executive Board meetings

without voting privileges.

SECTION 2: The members of the Executive Board sha1l be elected at the first unit

meeting after the National Conference, to take office the first of January

the following year.

SECTION 3: The Executive Board shall conduct, in conformity with Constitution, the

affairs of the chapter when it is not in session. A majority of the Executive

Board shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business

(a) It shall have the power to authorize the expenditure of money.

(b) It shall have the power to authorize the President to appoint the committees

to carry out the purposes of the chapter

(c) It shall have the power to make and amend the rules for its own procedures,

It shall keen a record of such rules.

(d) It shall have the power to fix the time and place of its meetings.

(e) It shall take such steps as may be necessary to carry out a program or

function determined by vote of the members and not otherwise provided

for.

SECTION 4: The officers shall have the power and perform the duties customarily

belonging to their respective offices and. especially:

(a) The President shall preside at all meetings, appoint committees, exercise

supervision over the affairs of the chapter and aid in coordinating the

work of this chapter with that chapter of the parent organization and

with other chapters.

SECTION 5: The President or the Executive Board shall be empowered to call special

meetings of the Board as the need may appear, or shall call such special

meetings upon a written request of the said Board..

(a) The Vice-President shall perform the duties of the President in the

event of absence or incapacity of the President.

(b) The Secretary shall keep a record of all the minutes of the said chapter

(c) The Treasurer shall collect any fees authorized by the Executive Board,

have custody of the funds of the chapter, make such expenditures as

may be authorized by the President with the advice and. counsel of the

Executive Board, and keep a record of all money received and

expended.

SECTION 6: The fiscal affairs of the chapter shall be audited annually at the close of

the business meeting in December, by three members appointed from the

membership by the Executive Board.

**ARTICLE VI: MEETINGS**

SECTION 1: There shall be monthly meetings of the organization at a time and. place to

be set by the Executive Board.

SECTION 2: Voting in the business meeting shall be by voice vote except for secret

ballot in the election of officers. One third of the members that constitute a

quorum.

SECTION 3: Written proxy votes will be acceptable at general membership meetings on

membership issues.

**ARTICLE VII:** **COMMITTFES**

SECTION 1: Committees are to be appointed by the President of the Executive Board as

the necessity arises.

SECTION 2: Standing committees shall consist of:

AWARDS: The purpose shall be to prepare nominations of individuals for

national or regional, awards; to develop and. awards program at the local

level; to nominate individuals as recipients.

CONSTITUTION and. BY-LAWS: The purpose shall be to maintain the

Constitution and By-Laws of the chapter ; to receive suggestions for

amendments or changes from the membership and to prepare

recommendations which conform with the national, and regional

Constitution and By-Laws.

PROFESSIONAL RELATIONS: The purpose shall be to develop means

of fostering professionalism; to promote increased participation of the

membership; to develop methods of communication and publicity of

chapter activities; to coordinate its activities with those of membership

chairperson and the NADE Advocate Reporter.

PROGRAM: The purpose shall be to plan programs for meetings of the

Chapter to assist in planning of programs for regional or nat1onal

meetings as needed; to recommend programs for special events.

**ARTICLE VIII: PARLIMENTURY AUTHORITY**

The rules contained in ‘Robert’s Rules of Order’ shall govern this organization in all cases to which they are applicable provided that if the said rules are in conflict with the Constitution of the National Association Of Disability Examiners, the latter shall govern.

**ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS**

The Constitution may be amended by a. two-thirds (2/3 standing vote of those members attending a. meeting, providing that a notice of change was sent to each member at least one week prior to the meeting.
