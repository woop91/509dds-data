**By Laws of the**

**Clarksburg West Virginia Association of Disability Examiners**

**ARTICLE I**

**NAME**

The name of this organization shall be the Clarksburg West Virginia Association

of Disability Examiners (CWVADE).

**ARTICLE II**

**AFFILIATION**

The Clarksburg West Virginia Association of Disability Examiners is a chapter of

the National Association of Disability Examiners (NADE).

**ARTICLE III**

**DEFINITIONS**

1. Association: The National Association of Disability Examiners.

2. Region: That geographical area comprising several states, including

the State of West Virginia, as designated by the

Association.

3. Chapter: The Clarksburg West Virginia Association of Disability

Examiners.

**ARTICLE IV**

**PURPOSE**

1. To advance the profession of disability insurance claims adjudication.

2. To promote the quality of service to the public by the profession.

3. To provide a forum for members’ concerns.

4. To provide a medium for social and professional interaction among members.

**ARTICLE V**

**MEMBERSHIP**

Membership in the Chapter shall consist of those members of the Association working or residing in the State of West Virginia.

**ARTICLE VI**

**FINANCES**

1. Dues. There shall be no dues except those for Association membership.
2. Fiscal and membership years shall coincide with those of the Association.
3. Rebates shall be requested by the Chapter in accordance with Regional policy.

**ARTICLE VII**

**EXECUTIVE BOARD**

1. The Chapter Executive Board shall consist of the officers of the Chapter, the immediate Past President, and the Representative to the Regional Board.
2. The Executive Board shall meet at the call of the President. Additional meetings may be called at the request of a majority of the Board.
3. The representative to the Regional Board shall be elected as called for in the Regional Constitution.

**ARTICLE VIII**

**OFFICERS**

1. The Chapter shall elect a President, Vice-President, and a Secretary-Treasurer on an annual basis.
2. Terms of office shall be for one year, with re-election permissible for only one additional year.
3. Officers shall perform those duties usually associated with their respective positions. Additional duties may be assigned by the President and/or Executive Board.

**ARTICLE IX**

**MEETINGS**

1. Meetings of the Chapter shall be held annually at a time and place to be determined by the Executive Board. Additional meetings may be held at the call of the President as necessary, and at the request of the majority of the members of the Chapter.
2. The meetings shall be governed by the latest edition of Roberts’ Rules of Order, insofar as they do not conflict with these bylaws or any rules adopted by the Executive Board of Membership.

**ARTICLE X**

**COMMITTEES**

1. Standing. At the annual business meeting, the incoming President shall announce, subject to confirmation by those present and voting, the Chairs of the following Committees:

Membership. This Committee shall plan the membership campaign and publicize Chapter and Regional and Association reports and materials.

Program. This committee shall work with the Executive Board in planning the activities of the Chapter.

Nominations. There shall be a nominating committee in the Chapter (consisting of at least three persons) which shall seek candidates of each of the elective offices.

1. Ad Hoc. The President may also appoint other special committees as necessary, with approval of the appropriate Executive Board.

**ARTICLE XI**

**AMENDMENTS**

1. An amendment may be presented by the majority of the Executive Board and shall become effective upon ratification by two-thirds of the members present and voting at any membership meeting.
2. An amendment may be proposed by a majority of members present at a membership meeting, which shall then be referred to an Ad Hoc Committee for study. Such amendments shall be presented at the next membership meeting, and may become effective when approved by a two-thirds vote.
