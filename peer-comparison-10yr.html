MA: −24% staff over 10 years · steepest drop in the peer set · accuracy −1.3 pts
Of the nine population-peer states (6–8M residents), only Massachusetts combines a steep staffing decline (−24.3% from 371 in 2014 to 281 in 2023) with a 10-year accuracy slip. Arizona and Virginia added staff (+13% and rebound to 427); MA never recovered. Accuracy held above the federal 90.6%/95% bar throughout — but MA peaked at 98.2% in 2016 and has stayed below 96.5% every year since 2019.
