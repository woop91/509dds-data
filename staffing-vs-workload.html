The squeeze on Massachusetts DDS-Det

Examiners and total staff are down ~40% from peak. Claims have rebounded to near pre-pandemic volume. National processing time has nearly doubled. Accuracy has held — but the gap between work-in vs hands-to-do-it is the largest in living memory.
Sources: SSA-SA-FYWL.csv (claim receipts), MA Comptroller CTHRU payroll (VDE + total staff), MA Section 9F annual reports (accuracy), SSA Combined Disability Processing Time CSV (national days) · Repo: github.com/Woop91/509dds-data · Generated 2026-05-04
−44% examiners · +27% claims since trough · workload-per-examiner up 73%
Vocational Disability Examiners fell from 232 (2019) to 129 (2023). Claim receipts rebounded from a 2021 low of 29,228 back to 37,055 in FY24 — within 4% of the 2019 peak. The arithmetic: each remaining examiner is now responsible for ~287 claims/year, up from ~166 in 2019. Decisions are still hitting the federal accuracy bar, but the pipeline behind them is breaking.

Examiner count, total staff, claims received, accuracy & national processing time · 2010–2025

Five series, four axes. Left axes: headcount (VDE + total staff) and adult claims received (in thousands). Right axes: national processing time in days, and MA accuracy of decisions (held above the federal 90.6%/95% bar throughout — values labeled inline). The 2016 staff bump is a CTHRU reclassification artifact, not a real hire wave — flagged on the chart. The visual story: red and orange (staff) collapse, blue (days) climbs, purple (claims) recovers from the 2021 trough — and green (accuracy) stays nearly flat across all of it.
