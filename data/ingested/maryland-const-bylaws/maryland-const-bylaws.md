# BYLAWS

MARYLAND CHAPTER OF THE NATIONAL ASSOCIATION OF DISABILITY EXAMINERS

# ARTICLE I – NAME

The name of this organization shall be the Maryland Association of Disability Examiners, also known by the acronym, MADE.

# ARTICLE II - AFFILATION

MADE is a Chapter of the Nationals Association of Disability Examiners (NADE).

# ARTICLE III – DEFINITIONS

Definitive terms are those terms used in the Bylaws of the Mid-Atlantic Region Association of Disability Adjudicators (MARADE).

1. Regional Board. The use of the term Regional Board shall refer to the Regional Chapter of the National Association to which this Chapter shall belong.
2. Association. This term shall be used in reference to the National Association of Disability Examiners.
3. Chapter. This term shall be used in reference to the Maryland Chapter of the National Association of Disability Examiners.

# ARTICLE IV – PURPOSE

1. To advance the profession of disability insurance claims adjudication.
2. To promote the quality of service rendered to the public.
3. To provide a forum for members’ concerns.

# ARTICLE V – MEMBERSHIP

Membership in this Chapter shall be open to all persons living or working in the State of Maryland other than those who are working at the Headquarters of the Social Security Administration. All members must meet the requirements of the National Association.

# ARTICLE VI – FINANCES AND MEMBERSHIP YEAR

1. Dues. There shall be no dues except those for Association membership.
2. Rebates. Rebate requests shall be signed by the Chapter Treasurer and requested of the Regional Chapter Treasurer between February 1 and March 31.
3. Membership Year. The membership year shall coincide with those of the Regional Chapter and the Association.

# ARTICLE VII – OFFICERS

1. Designated. The officers shall be a President, a Vice-President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer, and a Representative to the Regional Executive Board. All officers shall be Full Members of NADE.
2. Terms. The officers shall serve terms of one year, except the Representative to the Regional Executive Board, who shall serve a term of two years, or until the next Representative to the Regional Executive Board is elected. No officer may succeed himself for more than one additional term.
3. Elections. Elections shall be held annually during the third week of May. The term of office shall begin on June 1 and expire on May 31 of the following year.
4. Duties. The officers shall perform the duties traditionally associated with their offices, except as prescribed and restricted by the Bylaws.

a. President

1. 1. To call and preside over all meetings.

2. To appoint committees to the Chapter Executive Board

1. 3. To serve as a coordinator for the work of the Chapter with that of the Regional
2. Executive Board.

b. Vice-President

1. To perform the duties of the President in that person’s absence.

2. To assume the position of President in the event of the resignation or removal of

office of the President.

c. Secretary

1. To keep a record of the minutes of the meetings.

2. To post notice of all meetings and to handle Chapter correspondence.

d. Treasurer

1. To collect any fees found necessary and voted upon.

2. To have custody of the funds of the Chapter.

3. To keep a record of all money received and expended, and between February 1 and

March 31, request rebates from the Regional Chapter Treasurer.

e. Representative to the Regional Executive Board

1. To attend all Regional Executive Board meetings.

2. To disseminate information obtained to the Chapter.

3. To perform all duties prescribed by the office as defined in the Bylaws of the

Region (MARADE).

**ARTICLE VIII – EXECUTIVE BOARD**

1. Members. The Executive Board shall consist of the President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer, Representative to the Regional Executive Board, the Immediate Past-President and any Chapter member elected to Regional or National office.
2. Meetings. The meetings shall be called at least once per month by the President. Special meetings may be called by the President at the request of two or more of the Board members.
3. Duties. The duties shall be as follows:

a. To handle emergency decisions between meetings of the general membership.

b. To plan the agenda for the general meetings.

c. To discuss Regional and National issues.

d. To elect by 2/3 vote of the Board, vacancies in the positions of officers, except the

office of President, which may have occurred between elections.

e. To elect by 2/3 vote of the Board, a chairperson of a nominating committee by the first

week of April, preceding the May election.

f. To encourage any activities found necessary to fulfill the purposes of the Association.

4. Voting. Voting shall be open unless a motion for secret ballot is made and passed by 2/3 of those present. Each Board member shall have one vote.

**ARTICLE IX – COMMITTEES**

1. Standing. Within thirty days of assuming the Presidency, subject to Chapter Executive Board approval, chairpersons shall be named to the following committees.
2. Membership. The chairperson shall organize and direct the recruiting of the Chapter and make recommendations to the President with respect to such action.
3. Program. The chairperson shall plan, organize, and publicize programs of professional and educational interest for MADE members and other interested persons, and shall attempt to establish at least one program per year.
4. Ad Hoc. The President, with Executive Board approval, shall appoint special committees and their chairpersons when deemed necessary.

**ARTICLE X – MEETINGS**

1. Meetings shall be called at quarterly intervals. Additional meetings may be called at the discretion of the Executive Board. Two-thirds of those present shall constitute a majority.
2. Voting shall be open unless a motion for secret ballot is made and passed by 2/3 of those present. Each balloting for officers shall be held by secret ballot and a 2/3 vote of those present will carry issues.

**ARTICLE XI – PARLIMENTARY AUTHORITY**

The rules contained in Robert’s Rules of Order shall govern this organization in all cases to which they are applicable provided that if the said rules are in conflict with the Bylaws, the latter shall govern.

**ARTICLE XII – AMENDMENTS**

1. The Bylaws may be amended by 2/3 vote of those members in attendance, provided notices of the meeting and proposed Bylaw changes have been posted at least one week prior to the meeting to be considered as part of the business of that meeting.
2. Any amendment that is legally voted into action shall be effective immediately.
3. Written copies of such amendments shall be made available to each member within one month of the effectuation date.
