1/Allowance and denial rates equal 100%.
2/In prototype states, the reconsideration level of appeal does not usually apply, (the primary
exception is when a claimant has moved from a non-prototype state), so reconsideration data
from prototype states is not meaningful for analysis with non-prototype state data. Prototype
states indclude: Alabama, Alaska, California (LA north and west branches only), Colorado,
Michigan, Missouri, New Hampshire, New York, Louisiana and Pennsylvania. Reconsideration
cases completed by prototype sites are approximately 3.3% of the national total.
3/Includes Social Security only as there is no SSI in Puerto Rico.
4/ EST DDS data comes from four extended service team sites in Arkansas, Mississippi,
Oklahoma, and Virginia. Workload from State DDSs is transferred into these sites.
5/Federal Site data comes from the following SSA sites: Office of Medical/Vocational Expertise
(OMVE); Office of Central Operations; International Payment Service Center; Guam;
the Virgin Islands; and Disability Program Branches in the San Juan, Atlanta, Chicago, Dallas,
Kansas City, New York, Philadelphia, and San Francisco Regions. Determinations in the Denver,
Boston, and Seattle regional offices are currently counted in state DDS data due to systems
issues. Workload from State DDSs is transferred to these sites.
Note: Allowance rates are on determinations completed by State Disability Determination
Services (DDSs) and other components which make disability determinations. For workload
balancing issues, cases may be transferred to other states or to SSA components for
determinations. Determinations are not always done in the applicants' state of residence.
Allowance rates of 0.0 indicate that no claims were processed by the component in FY 2012.
