**Subject:** FOIA Request — Massachusetts DDS budget allocation, SAOR workload &
staffing, federal QA returns, and performance correspondence, FY2013–FY2024

**Body:**

Pursuant to the Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552, I request the
following records concerning the **Massachusetts Disability Determination Services
(DDS)** (administered by MassAbility, formerly the Massachusetts Rehabilitation
Commission), and, where indicated, all state DDS agencies.

### Section 1 — Massachusetts DDS budget allocation (the key federal-only item)

1. The **annual budget authorization / spending-authority** issued by SSA/ODD to
   the **Massachusetts DDS**, for each federal fiscal year **2013 through 2024** —
   i.e., the document(s) by which ODD allocates work-years and funding to MA DDS
   (the per-state allocation that is not published in SSA's Congressional
   Justification). Include any mid-year reallocations, hiring-freeze guidance, or
   overtime-funding authorizations specific to MA DDS.
2. The **federal reimbursement actually paid** to Massachusetts DDS (e.g., the
   SSA-4513 State DDS cost report or equivalent quarterly/annual reconciliation),
   FY2013–FY2024.

### Section 2 — State Agency Operations Report (SAOR) workload & staffing

For federal fiscal years **2019 through 2024**, for **each state DDS** (so MA can
be benchmarked):

3. SAOR workload data — initial / reconsideration / CDR / hearings receipts and
   dispositions; allowance rates; average processing time.
4. State-level staffing — **authorized examiner FTE**, total authorized DDS
   workforce, and any vacancy / attrition figures SSA tracks.

### Section 3 — Federal quality-assurance returns (the story behind the accuracy %)

5. For **Massachusetts**, the count of cases the SSA regional QA component
   **returned to the DDS for correction**, by federal fiscal year **2019–2024**,
   broken down by **deficiency type / reason** to the extent tracked. (The single
   published net-accuracy percentage hides this detail.)

### Section 4 — SSA ↔ Massachusetts DDS correspondence trail

6. SSA Boston Regional Office (Region I) **annual performance-feedback letters**
   or assessments sent to Massachusetts DDS, FY2019–FY2024.
7. Any **performance-improvement-plan**, corrective-action, or formal-concern
   notices issued by SSA to Massachusetts DDS, FY2019–FY2024.

### Section 5 — National systems affecting MA DDS operations

8. Records concerning the **incoming-fax failure rate** in the systems serving
   state DDS offices (including NCPS), and the **process by which a DDS requests
   that an NCPS letter template be edited** to reflect an updated fax number —
   including any analysis of fax-delivery failures or false delivery
   acknowledgments, **2023–present**.
9. Records concerning the **Disability Case Processing System (DCPS)** rollout,
   downtime, or known-defect issues **as they affected Massachusetts DDS**,
   **2020–present**.

### Section 6 — Oversight reports

10. SSA OIG audit **"Staffing, Productivity, and Processing Times at State
    Disability Determination Services" (072309)** and any successor or
    Massachusetts-specific audit, FY2019–FY2024.

## Background and public interest

The SSA–state-DDS partnership adjudicates roughly 1.9 million disability
determinations annually. Examiner attrition documented in OIG audit 072309
directly affects claimant wait times, which have grown sharply per SSA's own
published processing-time data. State-level budget allocation, QA-return detail,
and performance correspondence enable independent analysis of which states are
most affected — directly relevant to Congressional oversight, claimant advocacy,
and labor-management discussion at state DDS offices.
