SSA disability beneficiaries in Massachusetts (age 18–64)

Caseload of currently enrolled MA adults receiving SSDI/SSI benefits. Peaked at 304,168 in FY 2014; down to 224,106 in FY 2024 — a 26% decline that drives the falling claim volume seen in the next chart.
Source: SSA-SA-FYWL.csv · column "SSA Disability Beneficiaries age 18-64"

Adult claim receipts vs determinations

New claims filed (receipts) and decisions issued (determinations). Volumes mirror beneficiary trend — both have collapsed from FY 2010 peak.
Source: SSA-SA-FYWL.csv

Favorable determination rate

Share of decisions that grant benefits. Steady ~45% through FY 2009, fell to a 40% floor in 2012, recovered briefly during COVID, now back at the floor.
Source: SSA-SA-FYWL.csv · "Favorable Determination Rate" (all) and "Adult Favorable Determination Rate"

National processing time (days) — 19-year view

SSA does not publish state-level processing times. The national trend was stable at ~100–115 days for nearly a decade (FY08–FY18), then more than doubled. FY16's 110-day average was historically normal; FY24's 231 days is the anomaly. FY26 (through April) is partial.
Sources: SSA Combined-Disability-Processing-Time.csv (FY08–FY15) + fy16-onwards-CDP-Time-Monthly.csv (FY16–FY26 partial)

Cost per case (USD)

MassAbility's reported cost-per-case rose from $516 in FY 2013 to $1,071 in FY 2024 — a +107% jump, mostly post-2019 (staffing collapse + slower throughput).
Source: MA Section 9F annual reports (2013–2024 published; 2025 TBD)

VDE headcount by grade (CTHRU payroll)

Vocational Disability Examiners (Grades 20/21/23) per Comptroller payroll. Senior pipeline (VDE III, Grade 23) shrank from 44 to 7 — a collapse of the experienced workforce. Note: CTHRU is a payroll-records count and runs higher than SSA SAOR FTE figures.
Source: CTHRU MA Comptroller payroll · `chris='MRC'` filter · 2024–2025 data not yet released (~12mo lag)

DDS accuracy of decisions (federal QA)

Federal Quality Assurance review of MA DDS decisions. Standard raised from 90.6% to 95% in 2023 — MA has held above the new bar but with less margin than before.
Source: DDS-Net-Accuracy.csv (FY 2007–2022) + MA annual reports (2023–2025)

DDS-Det operating budget (USD)

Only FY 2023–FY 2025 is currently published (pre-FY23 pending PRR). FY 2024 → FY 2025 is a $8.5M cut (−16.7%) on top of the staffing collapse.
Source: MA legislative budget docs · pre-FY23 pending PRR (issue #1)

National continuing disability reviews processed

CDRs are recurring eligibility checks for existing beneficiaries — work that flows through every state DDS. National volume peaked at 2.29M cases in FY18, dropped 35% by FY20 (COVID + staffing), and has not recovered. Full Medical Reviews (the most labor-intensive subset) tell the same story. Splits the workload picture: even with new claim volume falling, total DDS load includes this CDR backlog.
Source: SSA Periodic-Continuing-Disability-Reviews.csv (national, FY13–FY22 — last updated 2023-05-22)
Disambiguation: "DDS" here means Disability Determination Services — a division of MassAbility (formerly MRC) that adjudicates SSDI/SSI claims for SSA. Not the Department of Developmental Services (a separate MA agency).
