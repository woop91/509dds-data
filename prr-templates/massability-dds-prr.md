**Subject:** Public Records Request — Disability Determination Services (DDS)
division operational, workforce, and budget records, FY2002–FY2025

**Body:**

Pursuant to the Massachusetts Public Records Law, M.G.L. c. 66, § 10, I request
the following records of the **Disability Determination Services (DDS) division**
of MassAbility (formerly the Massachusetts Rehabilitation Commission). Where a
fiscal-year range is given, please provide every year for which a responsive
record exists, even if some years are missing.

### Section 1 — Annual / Section 9F reports

1. The complete Annual Report and/or Section 9F report submitted by the DDS
   division to the Legislature for each fiscal year **2002 through 2022** (the
   years not currently published on mass.gov).

### Section 2 — Operational metrics, by fiscal year (FY2013–FY2025)

For each fiscal year, the DDS division's figures for:

2. Total claim receipts (initial + reconsideration + CDR + hearings)
3. Total claim dispositions (same scope)
4. Continuing Disability Review (CDR) receipts and dispositions
5. Consultative examinations (CE) purchased, and the CE rate as published
6. Medical Evidence of Record (MER) requests purchased, and the MER rate as published
7. Cost per case (per-disposition cost), with the calculation method used

*Note: I am specifically missing FY2021 and FY2022 for items 2–6, and the FY2025
figures once compiled. If those years are easiest to produce from the underlying
Section 9F worksheets, that is acceptable.*

### Section 3 — Two specific reconciliations

8. The **FY2022 favorable-determination ("% allowed") rate** as stated in the
   DDS Section 9F report, **and the underlying allowed-count and total-count**
   used to compute it. (A published MA figure of 57% diverges from SSA's FY2022
   figure of 46.76%; I am trying to reconcile the two.)
9. For the **homeless / unhoused population**, the **count** of disability claims
   processed for individuals identified as homeless or residing in a shelter, for
   each fiscal year **2013–2025**, together with the definition/method used to
   identify them. If only a rate is maintained (e.g., the 22% figure in the
   FY2023 report), please provide the **numerator and denominator** behind that
   rate. *(I am seeking a count, not a rate.)*

### Section 4 — Budget, by fiscal year (FY2013–FY2022)

10. The DDS division's annual operating budget (the federal SSA reimbursement
    received), for each fiscal year 2013–2022.
11. Any available breakdown of that budget by category (personnel, medical/
    psychological consultants, consultative-exam purchases, IT/systems, fixed
    costs).

### Section 5 — System & operational defects

12. Records sufficient to show the **incoming-fax failure rate** for DDS fax
    numbers, including: IT/helpdesk tickets, vendor or SSA/NCPS correspondence
    about fax-delivery failures or **false delivery acknowledgments** (where the
    system reports a fax received that was in fact dropped), and any analysis of
    fax reliability, **2023–present**. Also any records describing the process by
    which DDS can request that an NCPS letter template be edited to reflect an
    updated fax number.
13. **Case-processing system availability** records (DCPS / eCAT / MCS or the
    successor system): outage logs, scheduled- and unplanned-downtime records,
    and helpdesk ticket volumes affecting DDS, **2023–present**.
14. **Consultative-exam scheduling performance:** records sufficient to show CE
    no-show rates, cancellation rates, and vendor turnaround time (days from CE
    order to report received), **2020–present**.
15. **Medical-evidence turnaround:** records sufficient to show average days for
    providers to return requested MER and the count of outstanding/overdue MER
    requests, **2020–present**.
16. **Decision-notice mailing:** records sufficient to show outgoing
    decision-notice print/mail delays and returned-mail rates, **2020–present**.

### Section 6 — Caseload & assignment (the workload record)

17. All **directives, memoranda, or policies** setting a weekly **case-assignment
    cap or intake target** for examiners (including any 18-case and any 20-case
    figure), and any record describing the **"case weight" methodology** used to
    count assignments, **2023–present**.
18. Any **productivity-expectation** memoranda or standards issued to examiners,
    **2023–present**.
19. Records sufficient to show the **distribution of pending caseload per
    examiner** and the **count of aged cases** (e.g., pending beyond a defined
    threshold), by office (Boston, Worcester), **2023–present**. *(Aggregate or
    de-identified figures are acceptable; no individual claimant or employee
    identifiers are sought.)*

### Section 7 — Overtime

20. **Overtime authorization** records for DDS — the memoranda authorizing
    overtime, the stated justification, and any dollar or hour caps — by pay
    period or by authorization event, **2010–present**.
21. Records sufficient to show **overtime hours offered and overtime hours used**
    (or overtime expenditures) for the DDS division, annually, **2010–present**.

### Section 8 — Workforce

22. Records sufficient to show, by month or by year, the count of **authorized
    but unfilled** Vocational Disability Examiner positions, the **average
    time-to-fill**, and the count of **separations** with reason code
    (resignation / retirement / termination / transfer), **2015–present**.
23. Records sufficient to show **examiner-training** class sizes, trainee
    attrition, and time-to-certification (time for a new examiner to reach full
    casework), **2015–present**.
24. The **Form 30 position descriptions** for the Vocational Disability Examiner
    series (all grades) and for DDS first-line supervisors.
25. **Aggregate** counts (no PII) of **grievances** filed and their dispositions,
    and of **reasonable-accommodation** requests and their outcomes/average
    processing time, within the DDS division, **2018–present**.

### Section 9 — Special populations (continued)

26. Any **other special-population breakouts** (e.g., HIV/AIDS, veterans,
    compassionate-allowance, terminal-illness cases) disclosed in or compiled for
    DDS annual or Section 9F reporting, **2013–present**.

## Public-interest note (voluntary)

These records document workforce, workload, and adjudication-outcome trends at a
DDS division that MassAbility's own FY2025 reporting describes as facing
unprecedented staffing vacancies. Public visibility into how staffing, caseload
caps, system reliability, and overtime interact has direct relevance to
claimants' wait times and to the public understanding of how the federal-state
DDS partnership operates in Massachusetts. The underlying open dataset is at
github.com/Woop91/509dds-data.
