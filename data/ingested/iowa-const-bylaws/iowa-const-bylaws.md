BY-LAWS OF THE IOWA ASSOCIATION OF DISABILITY EXAMINERS

ARTICLE I. NAME
The name of this organization shall be the Iowa Association of Disability
Examiners.

ARTICLE II. AFFILIATION

The Iowa Association of Disability Examiners is a Chapter of the National
Association of Disability Examiners.

ARTICLE III. DEFINITIONS
1. Association: The National Association of Disability Examiners.

2. Region: That geographical area comprising several states, including the
State of Iowa as designated by the Association.

3. Chapter: The Iowa Association of Disability Examiners located in Des
Moines of the State Disability Agency.

ARTICLE IV. PURPOSE
1. To advance the profession of disability insurance claims adjudication.

2. To promote the quality of service to the public by the profession.

3. To provide a forum for members’ concerns.

4. To provide a medium for social and professional interaction among members.
ARTICLE V. MEMBERSHIP

Membership in the Chapter shall consist of those members of the Association
working or residing in the State (or geographical area) of lowa.

ARTICLE VI. FINANCES
1. Dues. There shall be no dues except those for Association membership.
2. Fiscal and membership years shall coincide with those of the Association.

3. Rebates shall be requested by the Chapter in accordance with Regional policy.
ARTICLE VIL. EXECUTIVE BOARD
1. The Chapter Executive Board shall consist of the officers of the Chapter, the
immediate Past President, and the Representative to the Regional Board.

2. The Branch Executive Board shall consist of the officers and one member at
Large (elected at the same time as the officers), and the immediate Past
President.

3. The Executive Board shall meet at the call of the President. Additional
meetings may be called at the request of a majority of the Board.

4. The Executive Board shall conduct the affairs of the Chapter meetings of the
membership and:

a. Shall adopt a budget and expend funds from the Treasury.
Shall fix the time and place of the meetings of the membership.

c. Shall fill by its own vote, officers or Board positions, which have
become vacant between elections.

d. Shall act only when a quorum is present, which shall be a majority of
the Board.

ARTICLE VIII. OFFICERS

1. The Chapter shall elect a Present, Vice-President, and a Secretary-Treasurer
on an annual basis. |

2. Terms of office shall be for one year, with reelection permissible for only one
additional year.

3. Officers shall perform those duties usually associated with their respective
positions. Additional duties may be assigned by the President and/or
Executive Board.

4. The Representative to the Regional Board shall be elected as called for in the
Regional Constitution.

ARTICLE IX. MEETINGS

1. Meetings of the Chapter shall be held annually at a time and place to be
determined by the Executive Board. Additional meetings may be held at the
call of the President as necessary, and at the request of the majority of the
members of the Chapter.

2. The meetings shall be governed by the latest edition of Robert’s Rules of
Order, insofar as they do not conflict with these bylaws or any rules adopted
by the Executive Board of Membership.

ARTICLE X. COMMITTIES
1. Standing. At the annual business meetings, the incoming President shall
announce, subject to confirmation by those present and voting, the Chairs of
the following committees:

a. Membership. This committee shall plan the membership campaign and
publicize Chapter, Regional Na Association reports and materials.
b. Program. This committee shall work with the Executive Board in
planning the activities of the Chapter.
c. Nominations. There shall be a nominating committee in the Chapter
(consisting of at least three persons) which shall seek candidates for
each of the elective officers.

2. Ad Hoc. The President may also appoint other special committees as
necessary, with approval of the appropriate Executive Board.

ARTICLE XI. AMMENDMENTS

1. An amendment may be presented by the majority of the Executive Board and
shall become effective upon ratification by two-thirds of the members present
and voting at any membership meeting.

2. An amendment may be protested by a majority of members present at a
membership meeting, which shall then be referred to an Ad Hoc Committee
for study. Such amendment shall be presented at the next membership
meeting, and may become effective when approved by a two-thirds vote.
