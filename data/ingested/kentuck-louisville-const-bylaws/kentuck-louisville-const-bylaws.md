**Constitution**

**Of the**

**Derby City Association of Disability Examiners**

**Article I: Name**

**The professional, fraternal, and non-profit organization shall be name the Derby City Association of Disability Examiners.**

**Article II: Affiliation**

**The Derby City Association of Disability Examiners is a Chapter of the National Association of Disability Examiners and subject to the provisions of the National Association of Disability Examiners Constitution and By-Laws pertaining to the Chapters.**

**Article III: Purpose**

**Section 1: To bring together in a professional and fraternal manner those**

**individuals engaged in the documentation and evaluation of**

**medical and/or vocational information submitted in an application**

**for disability benefits.**

**Section 2: To promote a better understanding by the general public and**

**other concerned professionals of the methodology utilized in the**

**total evaluation and amelioration of physical and/or mental**

**infirmities in order to better serve the disabled individual.**

**Section 3: To provide a forum for the discussion of problems related to the**

**documentation, evaluation, and adjudication of disability claims.**

**Article IV: Definitions (For the purpose of this Constitution)**

**Section 1: “Association” means the National Association of Disability**

**Examiners.**

**Section 2: “Chapter” means the Derby City Association of Disability**

**Examiners.**

**Section 3: “Derby City” means the boundaries of the State of Kentucky**

**covered by the Derby City Association of Disability Examiners.**

**Section 4: All references to Office and Officers shall refer to Chapter**

**offices and officers unless otherwise stated.**

**Article V: Membership**

**Section 1: Members of the Chapter must be members of the Association**

**and/or employed by a public or private agency engaged in the**

**documentation and/or evaluations of medical and/or vocational**

**information submitted in support of applications for disability**

**benefits. All members of the Chapter as of June 1 in a given year**

**shall be eligible to hold elective office and serve as members of the**

**Executive Board in the coming year.**

**Section 2: Powers of the Membership.**

**The membership of the chapter shall:**

1. **Elect the four (4) officers for the Chapter and the Chapter**

**Representative to the Association Delegate Assembly.**

1. **Adopt amendments to this Constitution as herein provided.**
2. **Be able to delegate to the Board of Directors any of its power**

**except election of representatives and adoption of amendments.**

**Article VI: Meetings**

**Section 1: There shall be an annual meeting of the membership of the**

**Chapter.**

**Section 2: The President may call a special meeting when deemed necessary**

**with concurrence of a majority of the Executive Board.**

**Article VII: Officers**

**Section 1: The officers of the Chapter shall be President, Vice-President,**

**Secretary, and Treasurer. All shall be elected annually for terms**

**of one (1) year, or until their successors are elected, and may not**

**serve in the same office for more than two (2) consecutive terms.**

**The duties of the officers shall be those usually pertaining to such**

**positions, including specific duties assigned to each officer as**

**specified in the By-Laws of this Chapter**

**Section 2: The Nominating Committee shall meet in April to nominate**

**candidates for the officers to take office on July 1st. The**

**candidates selected by the Nominating Committee shall be**

**presented to the Chapter for voting by written ballot with a**

**simple majority of the members responding within twenty-one (21)**

**days carrying each office.**

**Section 3: Terms of office shall be for one year, starting on July 1st and ending on June 30th.**

**Article VIII: Derby City Southeast Regional Committee Member**

**The Chapter shall elect a representative to the Southeast Regional Executive Committee of the National Association of Disability Examiners. The Derby City Southeast Regional Executive Committee Member shall be elected for a two (2) year term and may not succeed himself/herself in this office.**

**Article IX: Board of Directors**

**Section 1: Composition of the Board.**

**The Board of Directors shall consist of the four (4) Chapter**

**Officers and the immediate Past President.**

**Section 2: Powers of the Board of Directors.**

**The Board of Directors shall:**

1. **Sponsor meetings of the membership.**
2. **Adopt a budget and administer the business affairs of the Chapter.**
3. **Propose resolutions and other policy statements to the membership, Association, or other entity, for consideration.**
4. **Hear and act upon reports of all committees.**
5. **Authorize the appointment of special committees.**
6. **Establish fiscal procedures for the use of funds and payment expenses.**

**Section 3: Filling of Vacancies.**

**The Board of Directors shall have the authority to fill, by vote of**

**its members, offices which have become vacant between elections.**

**Section 4: Meetings.**

**The Board of Directors shall meet on a quarterly basis. The**

**president may, at his/her discretion, or acting upon written**

**request of three (3) Board members, call special meetings of the**

**Board. Such a meeting may be held by mail and shall be conducted**

**under regulations of the Board. Reasonable notice of all Board**

**meetings, including time and place, shall be given by the Secretary,**

**at the President’s direction, to all Board members. All Board**

**meetings shall be open to the membership.**

**Section 5: Quorum.**

**A majority of the members of the Board of Directors shall serve as**

**a quorum for the transaction of business.**

**Section 6: Terms of Office.**

**Members of the Board shall serve terms of office as specified in**

**Article VII.**

**Article X: Limitations**

**Section 1: No substantial part of the activities of the Chapter shall be the**

**carrying on of propaganda, and the Chapter will not participate**

**in or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of**

**statements) a political campaign on behalf of any candidate for**

**public office.**

**Section 2: Not withstanding any other provisions of these Articles, the**

**Chapter shall not carry on any other activities not permitted**

**to be carried on by a Section 501 (c) (6) of the Internal Revenue**

**Code of 1954 (or the corresponding provision of any future**

**Internal Revenue Law).**

**Article XI: By-Laws**

**To implement this Constitution, the Chapter shall adopt By-Laws consistent with this document.**

**Article XII: Amendments.**

1. **An amendment may be proposed by a member of the executive Board. It**

**shall be referred for study to the Constitution and By-Laws Committee. This committee shall report to the Executive Board within sixty (60) days. If approved by a majority of the total Executive Board, it shall be presented to the Chapter by written ballot. If the proposed amendment is ratified by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the members responding within thirty (30) days, it shall become effective immediately.**

1. **An amendment may be proposed by any member of the Chapter at any**

**Chapter meeting. If an amendment so proposed is approved by a majority of the Chapter members present and voting, it shall be referred for study to the Constitution and By-Laws Committee. This committee shall prepare a written report to submit to the Chapter members within sixty (60) days of the amendment proposal. Voting shall be by written ballot. If the proposed amendment is ratified by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the members responding within thirty (30) days, it shall become effective immediately.**

**By-Laws**

**Of The**

**Derby City Association of Disability Examiners**

**Article I: By-Laws**

**This Document shall be known as the By-Laws of the Derby City Association of Disability Examiners adopted pursuant to Article XI of their Constitution.**

**Article II: Definitions**

**The same definitions described in Article IV of the Constitution of this organization shall apply to this document.**

**Article III: Membership**

**Section 1: Membership in DCADE is restricted to employees of the Louisville Office of Disability Determinations or service providers who are located in the geographic region of Kentucky bordered on the east by I-65, on the south by the Western Kentucky Parkway to the Purchase Parkway south to Fulton, Ky., as well as those located in areas of Jefferson County not covered by the above, and are members in good standing of NADE. (“Good standing” means those whose membership fees are paid in full for the current year.)**

**Section 2: Only members in good standing shall be permitted to participate or attend annual or special meetings.**

**Section 3: The same definitions described in Article III of the By-Laws of the NADE Constitution shall apply to this document.**

**Article IV: Dues**

**Dues other than national dues for NADE shall not be charged for membership in the Derby City Association of Disability Examiners.**

**Article V: Duties of Officers**

**Section 1: Duties of the President – The duties of the President shall be to:**

1. **Serve in office for one (1) year.**
2. **Call and preside over official meetings of the Chapter and Executive Board.**
3. **Appoint, with approval of the Executive Board, membership to all standing and special committees.**
4. **Serve as ex-office member with voting privileges on all committees which he/she appoints, except the nominations and elections committee.**
5. **Appoint, with approval of the Executive Board, a parliamentarian to rule on parliamentary and constitutional questions.**

**Section 2: Duties of the Vice President- The duties of the Vice President shall be to:**

1. **Assume the duties of the President in his/her absence.**
2. **Serve as ex-office member with voting privileges on all standing committees.**
3. **Assume the duties of President should the position become vacant until the term ends.**

**Section 3: Duties of the Secretary- The duties of the Secretary shall be to:**

1. **Make a true and permanent record of the proceedings of all**

**official sessions of the Chapter and Executive Board.**

1. **Submit a request for rebate of dues to the Regional NADE**

**Board member based on the number of the active members in the Chapter as of June 30 of the year of his/her term of office.**

**Section 4: Duties of the Treasurer- The duties of the Treasurer shall be to:**

1. **Assume custody and accountability of all funds belonging to the Chapter.**
2. **Assume responsibility for disbursing funds on order of the Executive Board.**
3. **Present a current financial report at all meetings of the Executive Board, as well as the annual meeting of the Chapter.**

**Section 5: Duties of the Southeast Regional Executive Board Member- The duties of the Southeast Regional Executive Board Member shall be to:**

**Attend all meetings of the Regional Chapter and serve on any**

**committees to which he/she might be appointed by the Regional**

**President.**

**Article VI: Duties of the Executive Board**

**Section 1: Quorum- A majority of the Officers of the Chapter shall constitute a quorum for transaction of business.**

**Section 2: Voting- A majority of the Executive Board members present and voting shall carry all issues, except where otherwise designated by the Constitution and these By-Laws.**

**Article VII: Committees**

**Section 1: Appointment of Standing Committees- No later than sixty (60) days following election of officers of the Chapter, the President, subject to confirmation by the Executive Board, shall appoint the chairpersons and members of the following committees: Membership, Nominations and Elections, Constitution and By-Laws, Program, Publicity, Awards, Legislative, Certification and Historian.**

**Section 2: Duties of the Committees**

1. **Membership- The Membership Committee shall organize and direct recruitment of members for the Chapter and report to the Executive Board with respect to these activities.**
2. **Nominations and Elections- The Nominations and Elections Committee shall obtain slates of candidates for each office becoming vacant at the end of the year. The Committee will advise the membership of the candidates’ identities following the April committee meeting. The Committee shall conduct the election in accordance with the policies approved by the Executive Board; and shall perform other related duties as assigned by the Board.**
3. **Constitution and By-Laws- the Constitution and By-Laws Committee shall study the Constitution and By-Laws for possible revision or amendment, receive and study suggestions for revision or amendment, and present its recommendations to the Executive Board or membership.**
4. **Program**- **The Program Committee shall recommend to the Executive Board a comprehensive program activities for the Chapter, including planning of the annual meeting program for the year o fits appointment.**
5. **Publicity- The Publicity Committee shall be responsible for seeking out and submitting for publication in the NADE Advocate and other professional literature and information which is deemed to be of interest to members of this organization. It shall also be responsible for publicizing the time and place of the annual meetings.**
6. **Awards**- **The Awards Committee shall dispense information regarding the awards program and solicit nominations for awards and conduct selection procedures. After recipients are selected, the committee will co-ordinate with the President regarding announcements and presentation of awards to recipients.**
7. **Certification- The Certification Committee will receive and process applications for the “Certified Disability Examiner” awards.**
8. **Legislative- The Legislative Committee will be responsible for keeping the Board informed regarding legislation affecting this organization. The Committee will have responsibility for communicating the organization’s position on legislative matters.**
9. **Historian- The Historian shall receive, organize, and retain such records of past and present Chapter activities so as to provide documentation of the growth and history of the Chapter.**

**Article VIII: Voting (General Meeting)**

**A simple majority of the members of the Chapter present and voting shall carry all issues, except where otherwise designated by the Constitution and these By-Laws.**

**Article IX: Amendments**

**The same amendment procedure described in Article XII of the Constitution of this organization shall apply to this document, with the exception of ratification, which shall be decided by a simple majority.**

**Article X: Rules of Order**

**Robert’s Rules of Order shall govern meetings of the Chapter and the Executive Board when they are not in conflict with the Constitution and By-Laws of the Chapter or rules**
